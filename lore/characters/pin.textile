A slim young man of about 5'9" — fair-skinned, brown-haired, green-eyed, wiry at a hundred and forty-five pounds. He carries himself with a soldier's quiet economy; watchful, hard to read. A young warrior seeking community, purpose and answers: does his sword hold the spirit of his dead friend, or is he losing his mind?

* *Alignment:* Neutral
* *Personality:* Values stoicism; wary of intelligence.
* *Ideal:* To find a place within a community again.
* *Bond:* Believes the spirit of his dead friend lives in his longsword [[Hesh | Hesh]] — the blade that took Essen's life.
* *Flaw:* Will stand up for those weaker than him, no matter the risk or need.

Sold into slavery before he’d even learned to speak, the boy was in his early teens before knowing the warmth of family and kinship. On the run after lashing out and killing his master, he stole away with a band of mercenaries, the Black Bloods, and was soon enamoured with their life of battle, bloodshed, gambling and carousing. They gave him a name, his first: Pin, so called for his keen wit and tactical mind, hidden beneath a slim stature and quiet demeanour.

In an ever deepening quest for power and coin, their charismatic leader, Killig, pushed his men down dark and dangerous paths. The group splintered when he sold their skills to the **Mad King of [[World History | Corvus]]** (~**4217 SC**) — a northern city-state broken from [[The Magedom of Houd | Houd]] after the Seven Day War — who tasked them with quelling a rebel uprising. Despite misgivings, Pin owed everything to Killig, and he and his close friend Essen made a blood oath to stay until the bitter end.

That end came soon enough on the field of battle.

Pin awoke beneath a mound of corpses, those of his friends and comrades. Pulling himself from the burial pit, he gulped fresh air as his gaze settled on a pair of familiar eyes. Essen stared back, their head mounted on a stave, one of hundreds lining the battlefield.

Consumed with despair, Pin wandered in a daze, ignored by the corpse-looters, until he came upon a sword. Hesh, Essen’s blade. Pin took the weapon, a final gift from the family that lay in ruins around him. But as he lifted it, a vision raced through his mind: Hesh, wielded by a giant of a person, their features obscured in the shadow of memory, brought the weapon down on Essen’s neck.

“It stung,” a familiar voice hissed from the sword, “But only for a moment.”

Essen.

The sword continued to speak in low whispers, snatches and fragments here and there over the coming weeks, audible only to Pin. At times it was a comfort. But a dangerous one, for sorcery of any kind was to be feared. Perhaps he was going mad.

Seeking answers, though unsure if he could bring himself to ask the necessary questions, Pin set out for [[Sindrel | Sindrel]], the city of magic.

h3. The sword

Carries a plain longsword with old, careful maintenance - a soldier's blade, not a noble's showpiece. The guard bears worn knotwork that might be [[House Meness | Meness]] law-runes if you know what to look for.

Pin's longsword is a **misplaced Meness archive piece** — see [[Hesh | Hesh]].

* **Origin:** An adjutant officer's sidearm from the first century of [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] - commissioned when [[House Meness | House Meness]] still lent witness-weapons to the [[Osteomantic Archives | Osteomantic Archives]] for the *Hall of the First Watch* (founding-era law officers and garrison dead). Catalogue mark filed under Meness, not Samhain.
* **How Pin has it:** Left circulation centuries ago (battlefield recovery, lost-property chit never closed, or a contractor "temporary loan" that outlived everyone who signed). Pin acquired it **before the campaign** - treat as player backstory unless you establish otherwise in play.
* **Why the Archives care:** Caretakers still maintain the First Watch roster. The blade is **checked out without return**. They will attempt to recover it politely before they attempt violence.
* **Magic (depowered):** The sword was **abjuratively enchanted** - a Meness pattern that turned aside the first harm each day and remembered the force that struck it (a crude echo of founder-tier adaptation). A millennium outside [[Sindrel | Sindrel]]'s density, and decades more in the mundane world, has **drained it to mundane steel**. Pin may feel it is *almost* right - balance, temper - without knowing why.
