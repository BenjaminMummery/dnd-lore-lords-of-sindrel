Actually goldfinger in disguise.

h2. Role in the Anathemist Plot

Regnif d'Log is the spy cell's patsy. He entered [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] with his servants, [[:arouraios | Arouraios]] and [[:barnabus | Barnabus]], while [[:georges-de-selve | Georges de Selve]] and [[:jean-de-dinteville | Jean de Dinteville]] entered separately under merchant cover. d'Log is not the mastermind; [[:barnabus | Barnabus]] is the de-facto cell lead. d'Log's usefulness lies in his respectability, his money, and his carelessness.

d'Log's household gives [[:barnabus | Barnabus]] and Arouraios a reason to be in [[Sindrel | Sindrel]]. It gives them rooms, errands, appointments, servants' access, household accounts, and a plausible reason to conduct business with local specialists. When Barnabus deals with [[:kelferria-the-smith | Kelferria]] for enchanted armour, he does so while supposedly acting as d'Log's agent. This ties the transaction to a plausible wealthy patron rather than to the [[Anathemists | Anathemist]] cell itself.

In the sabotage campaign before the [[Ditchquer | Ditchquer]] closing ceremony, d'Log unknowingly provided cover for purchases, meetings, and movements. He authorised, signed, or failed to question arrangements made by [[:barnabus | Barnabus]]. He payed for unusually large quantities of charcoal under the impression that his lodgings were cold or poorly heated.

d'Log may possess documents, receipts, orders, or correspondence that incriminate his household while also showing his ignorance. He is useful to the conspiracy precisely because he leaves a paper trail that points at him before it points at [[:barnabus | Barnabus]].

During and after the [[Ditchquer | Ditchquer]] operation, d'Log's most important contribution is passive: his retinue remains a respectable shield. [[:barnabus | Barnabus]] is permitted to stay in [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] as part of d'Log's household until his master's business is concluded, leaving an Anathemist agent embedded in the city even after Georges and Jean depart.

h3. Investigative footprint

Evidence around d'Log should initially look damning but become less conclusive under pressure. His accounts, letters, and authorisations may connect him to armour, charcoal, and suspicious appointments, but his ignorance should be visible in the details. He may be vain, negligent, foolish, compromised, or easily managed, but he is not the operational leader.
