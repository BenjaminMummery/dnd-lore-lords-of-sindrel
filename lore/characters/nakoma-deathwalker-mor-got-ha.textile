h3. Description

Nakoma is a 8ft tall Goliath from one of the mountain clans. In looks she appears to be around mid 40s but she either will not tell people or does not remember her exact age. Her skin is a stony grey reminiscent of granite. She is covered in a collection of faded tattoos which have symbolic meaning to her clan but they have not been maintained so it appears that whichever clan she is/was a part of it has been a long time since she has been in contact with them. In addition to her tattoos she is covered in scars, some scars are small, but some are large an deadly looking. Her hair is made up of a combination of dreads and plaits with quills, feathers and metal adornments woven into them. Her manner of dress is similar to Native American warrirors, made of mostly leathers, and some bone pieces.

Since the 'incident' in Fallowhurst she also sports a sigil burned into the skin of her back which mirrors the one burnt into [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin's]] eye.

<!-- OP_BIO -->
There have been varied sightings and mentions of Deathwalker over the last few decades. No one appears to know exactly where she is from or her exact age. It is unknown how many Deathwalkers there have been from the Mor'got'ha clan. Indeed there is some speculation that the Deathwalker line might have some relation to Deathbringer, one of the most mightiest warriors from ancient history who was pivotal in the battle against the necromantic [[The Thaumocracy of Tellerin | Tellerites]] for the description of Deathbringers tattoos have a familial resemblance to Deathwalkers, and indeed the entire Deathwalker line. If this is the case then Deathwalker is descended from what might be the Goliath equivalent of royalty.

Deathwalker has only been recorded in a handful of major battles. Accounts disagree on which was most recent before her arrival in [[Sindrel | Sindrel]]; mercenary rolls and traveller gossip place her on several distant fields in the years before the pilgrimage routes turned toward the city. Since then she has been sighted traveling, sometimes alone, sometimes within a small group, but she never remains with any group too long.

Her most recent traveling companions seem to be an eclectic bunch.

h2. Backstory

Nakoma was born to the Mor'got'ha mountain clan. She distinguished herself early in the small skirmishes of her home range and soon left inter-clan squabbles behind, joining **GROWL** (Goliath Regiment Of War Leaders). There she became a respected commander in the **Combined Interspecies Battalion Against Evil Doers Army (CIBAEDA)**. Their gravest campaign was against the necromantic armies of the [[The Thaumocracy of Tellerin | Tellerites]]; on those fields she earned the name **Deathbringer**, for wherever she walked, death followed, and she led several battalions through impossible odds to victory.

*(Outsiders still speculate that the Deathwalker line descends from an ancient Deathbringer of legend. Nakoma knows the truth: she earned the name herself long before she took another.)*

Her renown placed her at **Brassmeade Bridge** when [[:nitherfang-gilfrarrin | Nitherfang Gilfrarrin]] came for her soul. The necromancer — ousted from his order for crimes most nefarious, and counted among the worst of his craft in living memory — had heard of her deeds and meant to press her into his army of the dead. The battle of Brassmeade Bridge is remembered as one of the bloodiest fights of the Wars of the Dead. Nakoma and a handful of goliath warriors held the crossing for **ten days**, turning back necromantic hosts and then their own fallen as they rose from the field. On the seventh day her oathsworn **Minara** was killed; on the tenth Nakoma faced Minara's resurrected corpse and could not bring herself to desecrate the body of the one she had sworn her life to. Instead she destroyed the bridge, letting the white waters of the Meade wash away the dead of both sides and force the necromantic army to seek another crossing.

That was not Nakoma's end. Even as the Tellerites withdrew, Nitherfang hunted down her body and bound her soul to his service as bodyguard and military adviser — a turning point for the necromantic armies. She served him for years as his executioner, drawing strength from rage at every act forced upon her until she finally broke his will, **beheaded him**, and took his **shrunken head** as a trophy. She still carries it on her belt.

The binding did not break with his death. For a time she lost herself slaughtering necromancers wherever she found them, convinced they all carried Nitherfang's evil and that was why she still walked the earth. Eventually she understood that everyone she knew was dying of age or battle while she remained, preserved like a corpse that would not lie down.

She sought out a **Deathsinger** of her people — one who tends the souls of the dead.

"Will I ever die?"
"One who is not alive cannot die. You walk the paths of the dead, but you are not one of them."

It was then she took the name **Deathwalker**. She spent many years with the **Deathsinger** who became her companion; that companionship was always shadowed by the knowledge that Deathwalker would outlast her. When the Deathsinger's time came, she confessed she had long known how Deathwalker might finally die, but had withheld it so they could remain together and so she would not have to tend Nakoma's soul before her own passing. To die, Deathwalker must bring a great evil back into the world and win the hardest battle of her life: **resurrect Nitherfang**, force him to release her soul from his spell, and destroy him again. The necromancers' code forbids raising one of their own; reputable houses would never revive the worst of their order for any price, and the death-markets offered no one with power or knowledge enough for a corpse so steeped in malice.

Emotionally betrayed and wrestling with the morality of the choice, Deathwalker wandered as a sellsword — aiding those who could pay and sometimes those who could not, depending on her mood. Starvation and the elements could not kill her; she saw kings rise and fall and even aided a rebellion or two, resigned to her fate. As magic faded across [[Arcan | Arcan]] she witnessed the decline firsthand. Then came word of [[Sindrel | Sindrel]], the last bastion of magic, and in hushed whispers, necromantic ruins within the city that might hold an answer to her eternal problem.
