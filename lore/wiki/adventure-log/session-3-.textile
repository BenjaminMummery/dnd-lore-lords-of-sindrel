h2. [[Fallowhurst College | Fallowhurst College]], the Revenge

_Afternoon of the 25th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

bq). For a brief moment, the sigil floats in the air, the blood flowing around it, coiling unnaturally. [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]] and [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Deathwalker]] both feel the twist - the moment that the magic bucks within Wilrin's grip like a striking snake, vicious and lethal. Deathwalker lunges forward in a desperate attempt to interpose herself between Wilrin and the danger he's unleashed. As she reaches him, the sigil collapses into itself, becomes a crimson point that explodes in a wave of blinding darkness.
<br>
As your sight returns, the first thing visible is a subtle red glow. Slowly your vision begins to clear, and you can make out Wilrin sprawled on his back, almost lost beneath the motionless, face-down form of [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Deathwalker]]. Burned into the right side of Wilrin's face and the scarred expanse of Deathwalker's back, raw and bloody copies of the sigil glow like hot iron. As the magical darkness dissipates so too does the glow, the symbols fading into vicious scars in the skin of your friends.
<br>
The scream of incoherent rage that accompanied Wilrin's ill-fated casting echoes within the empty halls of the college, being replaced by the sounds of shuffling movement growing closer.

[[:silrie-aegiskiir | Silrie]] and [[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]] reached the fallen pair first. [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Deathwalker]] appeared quite dead; [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]] retained a thread of life. The two were dragged back into the museum while [[:pin | Pin]] shoved a display cabinet into the corridor to barricade the approach. Something heavy thudded against the other side.

[[:silrie-aegiskiir | Silrie]] smashed a stained-glass window with their staff. [[:pin | Pin]] smashed another. The pair climbed out and waited in the cold air while the fight continued indoors.

Within the museum, [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Deathwalker]]'s wounds began to knit themselves closed - the necromantic engine that sustained her rebooting in its own time. An arm wielding a scalpel punched through the splintered barricade. [[:ayr | Ayr]] lunged to strike it and missed; the blade found his arm instead, leaving a wound that radiated a steady, spreading numbness. [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]], spotting the enchanted scalpel, tried to snatch it with Mage Hand. The spell fumbled the grip and the blade fell to the floor. [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]] slid past the flailing arm in a move better suited to a concert stage than a haunted museum, seized the scalpel, and ran.

[[:amanira | Amanira]], watching cloaked figures advance through the dark lecture hall, spoke with the calm of someone who had decided panic was a waste of breath: they needed to leave. Now.

[[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]] dropped a rope through the broken window. One by one - [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Deathwalker]], [[:amanira | Amanira]], [[:ayr | Ayr]], and [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]] - the party climbed out into the grey afternoon.

h2. The Escape

_Late afternoon of the 25th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

The group fled along a narrow passage between the college and the flooded river, keeping the water in reserve should the way ahead be blocked. They had almost reached the entrance when the two cloaked pursuers smashed through the lecture-hall door and met them in the open.

[[:silrie-aegiskiir | Silrie]] attempted an elegant flip over the figures and landed at their feet instead, which proved an excellent position from which to crack a telescopic staff upward into a jaw and send a hood tumbling away, revealing bare bone beneath.

What followed was less elegant than decisive. Blades, spells, and improvised violence reduced one skeleton to a shorter, more compact version of itself when a bolt punched through its sternum and the bones settled with a crunch. [[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]] attempted a rope-assisted axe throw of cinematic ambition and missed entirely. The second scholar exploded in a shower of ivory that suggested the party had, at minimum, exceeded the building's recommended occupancy for animated remains.

[[:amanira | Amanira]] gathered bone fragments and a skeletal hand into a pouch for later study. The college, for the moment, had had enough of them.

h2. Regroup

_Late afternoon of the 25th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

Back at the wheelbarrow, [[:dulcinia-von-phthasia | Dulcinia]] was awake and inclined to wander. [[:pthul | Pthul]] was torn between rushing toward the sounds of battle and remaining with his charge. [[:raymond-quill | Raymond]] and [[:gillette-quill | Gillette]] were deep in conversation with the [[:jean-de-dinteville | merc]][[:georges-de-selve | hants]], pitching a lifestyle course and meditation retreat with the confidence of men who had recently discovered philosophy.

[[:silrie-aegiskiir | Silrie]] politely observed that there remained considerable work to be done on realising the Self. The party gathered their companions and moved on, some vowing to return when they were stronger and the building was, if possible, less actively homicidal.

h2. Leaving [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]]

_Evening of the 25th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

bq). The road is in better shape on this side of the valley, and you make swift progress as it winds its way uphill. The ominous forms of [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]] are soon obscured by dense woodland and your mood gradually brightens.
<br>
After some time, you emerge from the tree-line and find yourselves looking at a [[Fallowhurst Outpost | small, round tower]]. Unlike the decorative fortification of [[Fallowhurst College | Fallowhurst College]], this is clearly a very real defensive structure - recently fortified, by the look of it. What windows there are are small and high; the upper sections bristle with crenellations and murder holes, and a shimmer of magical shielding plays across the stonework. Surrounding the tower is a steep-sided ditch cut by mould-earth magic, the inner edge an overhang of dagger-like rock. The only way across is a narrow wooden drawbridge.
<br>
Next to the heavy studded door of the tower, stone hangs in the air. Roughly a meter high and shaped like two tall octagonal pyramids stuck base-to-base, you recognise it as one of the [[The Mage Road | Mage Road]] mile-markers - the first you have seen still floating, though ruined examples littered the route behind you, including between the gates of the Fallowhurst estate.
<br>
As you approach the tower a surprised voice addresses you:
<br>
"Halt! Who goes there?"

The outpost existed to ensure that nobody from [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] wandered into [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]] by mistake. It was crewed by [[:sebastian-warwick | Sebastian Warwick]], an [[Adjutant | adjutant]] of [[House Meness | House Meness]], and [[:myrath | Myrath]] of [[House Lithra | House Lithra]]. [[:sebastian-warwick | Warwick]] knew this posting for what it was - unofficial punishment after he had embarrassed a superior officer. [[:myrath | Myrath]], aware that the billet was a hard one to fill, had volunteered in hope of earning recognition from her House. The tower flew the flag of [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] - an eight-pointed star - and the garrison could summon transport or reinforcements from the city if the need arose.

The party were invited to sleep in real beds, each marked with a symbol - some an eye, some the oval and cross seen at the college - whose meaning remained unclear. They slept well, and woke to breakfast. [[:ayr | Ayr]], in particular, was delighted, and spent the meal extracting what history he could between mouthfuls of bacon. Much of the older kit in the tower bore the stamp of [[House Oestra | House Oestra]]; the newer issue carried the sign of [[House Meness | House Meness]] - a U on a stick with two cross-bars below.

Warwick explained that [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]] village had been empty for centuries. Travellers had begun to vanish; eventually suspicion fell on the college, the old route was closed, and a new road and bridge were built to bypass the valley. The outpost stood to stop Sindrelians meeting the same fate, though Warwick noted that nobody had come from the city that way in living memory. Coming through [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]] alive, he conceded, was no small thing. A posting here, he admitted without much bitterness, was widely understood to be a punishment.

Over the evening [[The Adventurers | the adventurers]] heard of the eight schools of magic, the founding of [[Sindrel | Sindrel]], and the Lord who had sought to betray the others. By morning, everyone had gained a level of experience they were keen not to repeat.

h2. The Road to [[Sindrel | Sindrel]]

_Morning through afternoon of the 26th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

bq). Those of you who have walked in this world for some time have a pretty clear idea of what cities look like. For centuries, large conurbations have tended to shrink as the political, economic, and ecological systems that supported their population density die out with the loss of magic. "City", therefore, conjures images of crumbling civic centres nestled in shells of abandoned suburbs, or even of the corpses of towns being cannibalised for building materials for the smaller, more self-contained communities that are beginning to develop. None of this is true for [[Sindrel | Sindrel]].
<br>
Sindrel sits on a high promontory jutting far out over the [[Norward Sea | Norward Sea]]. Even the city's _location_ is impossible - the barren, mountainous ground supports no farmland that could feed it, nor is there anything resembling a source of fresh water here. But that is not what captures your eye nor fires your imagination. Banners bearing the device of an eight pointed star hang from high white walls, above which rise a majestic profusion of spires and plazas. The city is clearly, ostentatiously magical. In a world where the smallest illusion can be the talk of the town, Sindrel's use of magical power is breathtakingly profligate. Above the walls, rising spires become lost in the structures that hang _down_ from above - buildings that float impossibly in the air, constructed to mirror themselves vertically in a way that defies any idea of terrestrial construction. Between and above them pass airships, great vessels with gilded prows and shimmering sails that glide effortlessly through open air with no visible means of support.

Figures appeared on the road ahead: five soldiers on horseback. [[Anathemists | Anathemists]], almost certainly. When they spotted the party their course changed at once. One consulted a scrolled paper, looked at the group, looked at the paper again, looked at a companion, looked at the paper once more, and then that companion took the paper and galloped back toward the bridge.

[[:silrie-aegiskiir | Silrie]] misty-stepped onto the rear of the fleeing rider's horse, grabbed him, and pulled him from the saddle before he had time to object to the occupancy of his personal space. [[:amanira | Amanira]] melted into the roadside cover.

The remaining patrol closed. [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]] cast a spell; wild magic spawned a smaller echo of himself that hurled toward the nearest soldier. [[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]] spoke to two of the horses and persuaded them that their riders were poor career choices; one hoofed its soldier and bolted. [[:pin | Pin]] brought down another by targeting the mount. [[:amanira | Amanira]] stepped from cover to attack.

The wild-magic echo locked onto a soldier who countered it with a spell of his own, sprouting tiny wings from its ankles and launching it skyward moments before it exploded like a firework. [[:silrie-aegiskiir | Silrie]], returning to the fray, arrived in time to admire the display.

Two [[Anathemists | Anathemists]] remained in the party's hands. What to do with them was a question for another moment.
