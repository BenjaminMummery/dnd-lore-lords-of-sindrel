h2. Over the Edge

_Afternoon of the 25th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

bq). As the [[:sindrel-coach | coach]] teeters on the edge of the cliff, a moment of stillness falls over the scene. With the echoes of the [[:commander-wake | Anathemist Commander]]'s weapon ringing in your ears, you have a chance to assess the battle. Two things become pressingly apparent. 
First, this fight is not winnable - the [[Anathemists | Anathemists]] have you outnumbered with reinforcements incoming, and whatever that weapon is has just turned an experienced and heavily armoured warrior into a red mist in an instant. 
Second, the coach's centre of gravity is too far out - one way or another it is going over the cliff. As it is, it will roll edgewise down the steep slope, dashing itself to splinters on the rocks and scree and almost certainly killing everyone within. If you manage to turn it there is a chance it will stay on its wheels, and small but promising chance that it will make it to the bottom intact.

[[:silrie-aegiskiir | Silrie]] misty stepped from the top of the cliff to take the reigns of the [[:sindrel-coach | coach]] - even with the horses gone they could still be used to turn the front wheels, providing a modicum of steering. [[:ayr | Ayr]] leapt from the top of the cutting to be caught by [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha |Deathwalker]] (with some assistance from [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]]'s mage hand). [[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]] succeeded in tying a rope to the railing atop the coach, providing herself, [[:pin | Pin]], and [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]] with a way to secure themselves. These, combined with [[:amanira | Amanira]]'s use of ensnaring vines was sufficient to mitigate the descent of the coach, turning a death-drop into a survivable, if rough, ride. With the furious and despairing cries of the [[Anathemists | Anathemists]] and [[The Vermillion Company | Mercenaries]] respectively fading behind them, the [[:sindrel-coach | coach]] and all of its passengers went plunging over the edge of the cliff.

The coach came to rest at the bottom of the slope in a boggy marsh under sparse tree cover. Miraculously, injuries were minimal - the only notable casualties were [[:pthul | Pthul]] who was cradling an obviously broken arm, and [[:dulcinia-von-phthasia | Dulcinia]] who had fainted. Despite [[:pthul | Pthul]]'s insistence that he was fine and that all medical attention should be on [[:dulcinia-von-phthasia | Dulcinia]], [[:silrie-aegiskiir | Silrie]] did some sneaky healing on him.

Unwilling to leave either [[:dulcinia-von-phthasia | Dulcinia]] or the gold with which to pay for her treatment, [[:pthul | Pthul]] offered [[The Adventurers | the adventurers]] 100 GP up front and more to follow to escort himself, [[:dulcinia-von-phthasia | Dulcinia]], and their luggage to [[Sindrel | Sindrel]]. [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Deathwalker]] erected a series of sharpened spikes positioned to cause a very bad day for anyone following the coach down the slope while the rest of the party cannibalised the remains of the coach to build a rudimentary wheelbarrow to transport the gold and the still-unconsious [[:dulcinia-von-phthasia | Dulcinia]]. 

A short distance away they found the remains of a road with a signpost pointing north-east to [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]]. Rotating the sign to further throw off pursuers, they continued in that direction.

h2. Fallowhurst

[[File:1543101  | class=media-item-align-left | 600px | Fallowhurst_img.png]]


bq). [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]] was once a moderately sized village on the bank of the Deren river comprised mostly of one- or two-storey wooden houses. What remains are little more than ruins - the bones of the houses, mouldering beams and posts, sticking jaggedly from the water. Here and there a more intact building retains some stairs, even some half collapsed roofs can be seen among the hollow corpses of what were once homes. 
<br>
The original course of the river can be seen in the remains of a broad levee that marks the northern edge of the village. Broad gaps mark where the levee has failed, allowing the river waters to flood through.
<br>
A wide stone bridge rises out of the shallow water, its first supports forming part of the crumbling levee. Broken stumps along the balustrade hint at long gone statues. On the far side of the river you can see the walls of a grand estate. Where they meet the bridge, a gatehouse stands, its crenelated turrets rising imposingly into the grey sky. To the left, the tops of barren trees are visible above the wall, reaching upwards like bony hands. To the right, a stone jetty stands several feet too high for the current water level. Stairs lead up to a broad gap in the walls, neatly framing an imposing manor house veiled in ivy. 

Examining the ruined houses, [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Deathwalker]] concluded that they had been abandoned for something on the order of centuries.

As the party moved through the village, they became aware of footsteps among the houses. These were revealed to be a [[:embren-oestra | figure in plague doctor garb]] - tall, thin, and wielding a cane. They ignored all hails and queries from the party, instead examining  the arrows embedded in [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | DW]]'s hands and directing them to the estate, saying that they could "fix you right up." Without saying another word, they strode off towards the manor house.

At [[:ayr | Ayr]]'s suggestion, the party searched the village. They found nothing of value, concluding that either the inhabitants took their possessions with them, or looters had picked the place clean in the intervening centuries. 

Proceeding towards the bridge, it becomes apparent that the road continues through the estate and towards [[Sindrel | Sindrel]]. The "fortifications" were entirely for show - whoever built this place did so as a show of power, lording it over [[Sindrel | Sindrel]]-bound travellers. Above the arch of the gatehouse are the remains of a stone crest, too weathered to be made out.

The [[:embren-oestra | plague doctor]] had headed to the right, into the manor house. Hearing a plaintive cry, [[:ayr | Ayr]], [[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Rossin "Ros" Greyhirst]], [[:amanira | Amanira]], and [[:silrie-aegiskiir | Silrie Aegiskiir]] decided to investigate the orchard on the left first. 

h2. The Orchard

[[File:1544300  | class=media-item-align-right | 600px | orchard.jpg]]

bq). Nothing much grows well in the hard soil of the [[Arkay Mountain Range | Arkay mountains]], and the fruit trees here are no exception. Gnarled and twisted trunks dot the orchard, a semi-random layout meant to evoke a forrest while providing broad pathways between. A rich persons impression of nature.
<br>
Between the trees you can make out the remains of neat beds, their regimented layout at odds with the pseudorandom distribution of trees. Many of these are almost lost beneath a thicket of tenacious growth.

Things did not go well in the orchard. 

A patch of [[Razorgrass | Razorgrass]] caused issues for [[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]] and [[:ayr | Ayr]] (the latter requiring some emergency attention from [[:amanira | Amanira]] to restore his moustache). [[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]]'s bad luck continued as she had a run in with some [[Strangle Vines | Strangle Vines]], prompting [[:silrie-aegiskiir | Silrie]] to cast sacred flame to save her. Hearing the commotion, [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | DW]] charged in and was snared by a second [[Strangle Vines | Strangle Vine]], ultimately being saved by a firebolt from [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]]. 

Managing to avoid the [[Sporeshrooms | Sporeshrooms]], the now reunited party made their way to the crying corner. There they encountered a [[:tama | giant stoat]] caught by a third [[Strangle Vines | Strangle Vine]]. Working together, [[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]] and [[:ayr | Ayr]] were able to free the creature, which recovered when given some food and warmed in a blanket. With a thankful squeak, it vanished through a crack in the orchard wall. 

Meanwhile [[:amanira | Amanira]] and [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | DW]] went to check out the greenhouses. There they encountered a number of [[Triffid | Triffids]] - larger and more aggressive variants of the small plants with which they were familiar. Pursued by [[Triffid | Triffids]], the adventurers exited the orchard.

h2. Fallowhurst College

[[File:1544298  | class=media-item-align-left | 600px | Byrgenwerth_concept_art_1.webp]]


Following the [[:embren-oestra | Plague Doctor]] towards the manor, the party observed a crest above the door - a kite shield featuring a symbol like a quartered oval and a scroll in [[Nital | Nital]] reading "Impetus Est Optima Protectio" - translated my [[:amanira | Amanira]] as "the best defence is a good offence". Beside the door is a  plaque with a much more accessible inscription in common - [[Fallowhurst College | "The Fallowhurst College of Medicinal Study"]].

h3. Entrance Hall (A)

Almost all of the windows were broken, and the interior was full of decaying dead leaves that have blown in from outside. Potted plants that once adorned corners had now grown wild. There was a clear path through the grime and debris as if someone has walked the same route over and over.

One wall of the entrance hall was decorated with a brass version of the shield crest, this one rent by a massive scar, the edges warped as if by an immense heat. 

h3. Preparation Room (B)

The party follow the trail into the Preparation Room. Besides some rusted gurneys and preserved specemins, the only objects of interest are two articulated skeletons on metal stands - one elvish and the other dragonborn. [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]] purloined the dragonborn skull. 

h3. Lecture Hall (C )

[[File:1544264  | class=media-item-align-right | 300px | fallowhurst_sigil.jpeg]]

Rather than follow the trail, the party chose instead to explore the other door leading off the preparation room. 

bq). This room runs the full three-storey height of the building, and is ringed by a pair of partially collapsed galleries accessed from the upper floors. Tall windows on the west wall once allowed the sun to illuminate the space, but their vacant frames have been almost overtaken by the ivy that once grew on the outside wall and is now colonising the interior. In what little light is admitted illuminates the remains of wooden pew-like benches that litter the floor. At the centre is a portable lectern and blackboard, whatever writing it once bore long-since washed away by the water dripping from a large crack in the vaulted ceiling.  

While [[:ayr | Ayr]] investigated the corner door leading outside, [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | DW]] and [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]] examined the blackboard. On the reverse side, they found an [[The Fallowhurst Sigil | incomplete magic circle]], something to do with a binding spell. For reasons best known to themselves, the pair proceeded to experiment with drawing the circle, producing a series of random magical side effects including a slight intoxication for [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]] and a small translocation. [[:ayr | Ayr]] broke the door, taking the doorknob with him.

h3. Museum (E)

Taking a look through the door to the north, [[:silrie-aegiskiir | Silrie]] encountered a grisly sight.

bq). Glass display cabinets once lined the walls of this room, the shattered shards of which crunch underfoot as you enter. Whatever was once displayed here has been removed - your eyes find a tall heap of tarnished silver wear, a mulchy strata that may once have been paper, and the splintered forms of various delicate and once expensive objects in the south-west corner. In their place, the remains of the cabinets play host to a collection of rotten bodies. Corpses of various sizes and condition are arrayed for your perusal. Close to the door they are comparatively fresh, one even retains sallow flesh stretched taught over its bones. Further in they are increasingly decayed - exposed bones clad in a new life of fungus and infestation.
At the far end of the room, beneath three shattered windows, is displayed a final body. Surrounded by rot and decay it is pristine. A young man, no more than 25.

Things did not go well _at all_ in the museum.

It is here that [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Deathwalker]] found part of the [[:map-of-fallowhurst-college-1 | Map of Fallowhurst College]]. From this they learned that the trail in the grime was leading to the Operating Theatre (D).

Examining the "displays" revealed the following:
* Each body was accompanied by a "label" - small piece of wood with markings on it. 
* Close to the entrance where the bodies are newest the writing on the labels was an illegible scrawl. As they approached the older corpses the writing became more legible.
* Each legible label included an experiment number, a name, a description of an injury or disease, and an outcome. All except the pristine body were marked as 'failure'.
* The pristine body was labelled _"Subject 1: Carlos Winterborn. 25 M. Occupation: Noble. Affliction: Cervical Fracture. Outcome: Partial Success."_ (Due to a bad roll, [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | DW]] is convinced this mad died of a venereal disease).
* [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]] determined that the body had in some way been magically altered.
* While the village was emptied centuries ago, the newest bodies are mere decades old.

[[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]] decided to try and recreate the [[The Fallowhurst Sigil | binding sigil]] on the body. The discharge of magical energy healed both him and [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | DW]], causing the latter to start bleeding profusely from her multiple wounds. It also caused some ominous chanting to start emanating from the Operating Theatre (D). 

Hearing both the chanting and the sound of movement, [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]] made the curious decision to draw the [[The Fallowhurst Sigil | sigil]] in the air using [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Deathwalker]]'s blood. The consequences of using blood magic to connect the two of them to a spell of such prodigious power as to have maintained itself for several centuries while magic itself died out remain to be seen.

h1. Plan

* everyone falls off the cliff
* [[:pthul | Pthul]] will intend to stay with the carriage with [[:dulcinia-von-phthasia | Dulcinia]] until help arrives - not least because they need the gold from their luggage to buy treatment, and it's too heavy to carry.
* Those journeying through Fallowhurst will meet [[:embren-oestra | Embren Oestra]] dressed as a plague doctor. He will assure them he can fix whatever malady they have, and attempt to lead them to the college. [[:embren-oestra | Embren]] speaks in shallow phrases left over from his life - "We'll get that fixed right up." "The best care on the continent.", etc.
* if they follow, he'll try to take someone to the operating theatre, strap them down, and begin the binding procedure. Pretty sure there's enough red flags that they're not going to go for that.
* if they don't he'll proceed on his 'rounds' - visiting the houses in the village. Once out of sight, he'll start tracking the party looking for an opportunity to get one of them alone and capture them.
* From investigating the village they may be able to learn that the people left of their own volition (no valuables left behind, no sign of break-ins, etc.).
* Crossing the bridge they'll see the Oestra crest, and can infer the power play going on from building a symbolic gate over a main road (they may recall the hard turn the coach took and conclude that this used to be the main route to Sindrel).

h2. Fallbacks:

- if they get everyone out of the coach, the [[Anathemists | Anathemists]] will decide not take them prisoner, dragging them back down the road to work they way down to the coach.
- if they try to press through Fallowhurst without exploring the college, [[:embren-oestra | Embren]] will grow desperate and attempt to capture the person at the back of the marching order.


h1. The Village



bq). You're standing amidst the remains of a moderately sized village.

Things of importance:
- The village is flooded to a depth of 3 feet.
- The estate is above the water, protected by the raised foundations.
- Almost all of the houses have collapsed, leaving crumbling walls and rotting roof beams in their place. 
- Players searching the houses find nothing of value - people left here voluntarily and took what they could with them, and those that _were_ taken against their will left behind enough people to collect their belongings. In several places, the remains of furniture and planks nailed across the insides of windows indicate the presence of makeshift barricades, as though the inhabitants were trying to keep something out.
- [[:embren-oestra | Embren]] is out and about

h1. The Bridge

bq). A wide stone bridge rises out of the shallow water, its first supports forming part of the crumbling levee. Broken stumps along the balustrade hint at long gone statues. On the far side of the river you can see the walls of a grand estate. Where they meet the bridge, a gatehouse stands, its crenelated turrets rising imposingly into the grey sky. To the left, the tops of barren trees are visible above the wall, reaching upwards like bony hands. To the right, a stone jetty stands several feet too high for the current water level. Stairs lead up to a broad gap in the walls, neatly framing an imposing manor house veiled in ivy. 

The bridge was designed to be wide enough for carriages to pass in both directions. It is sturdy, but has crumbled in a few places. Potential dex save to keep your balance.
Anyone paying attention will realise that the crenellations and arrow slits are too small and badly placed to be defensively useful, and either way the jetty is completely indefensible - the gate exists as a symbol of power.
Above its arch are the crumbling remains of the crest of house Oestra.

After passing through the gate, the road continues, flanked by high walls on each side, until it exits the estate heading North-East.

h1. The Orchard

* The "orchard" was originally planted for aesthetics - the trees are laid out in a pseudo-random pattern, mimicking the appearance of a forest but with wide, easily navigable spaces between. Later, it was repurposed to grow medicinal herbs - a grid pattern of neat ordered beds has been constructed, and you can almost sense the gardener's frustration at having to accommodate the trees. 
* The greenhouses were likewise repurposed. 
* Little grows well in the rocky soil of the Arcay range, and the trees are twisted and stunted. Much of the original inhabitants of the herb beds have died out without tending, however the more aggressive species have grown rampant and dangerous. Dangers of the orchard include:

* [[Strangle Vines | Strangle vines]] - hang from the trees and will attempt to entangle and crush anything the comes close.
* [[Razorgrass | Razorgrass]]. A carnivorous plant that consumes the blood drawn by its bladed leaves. Characters moving through a patch of razorgrass can either move at half speed, or take 1d4 slashing damage per 5 feet.
* [[Sporeshrooms | Sporeshrooms]]. Release poisonous gas when disturbed. Can result in paralyse, poison damage over time, blindness, etc

The greenhouses have been colonised by [[Triffid | triffid]] saplings. Most of these are immature and harmless, but one has reached maturity and can attack.

- The orchard is also home to [[:tama | Tama]].

h1. The College

The college was originally a manor house, repurposed into a hospital and training college for healers.

Almost all of the windows are broken, and the interior is full of decaying dead leaves that have blown in from outside. Potted plants that once adorned corners have grown wild, and a number of birds and other creatures have colonised the space.
