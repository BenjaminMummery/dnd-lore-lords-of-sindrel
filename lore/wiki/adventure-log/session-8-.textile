h3. The Crossroads

_Afternoon of the 29th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

[[:ayr | Ayr]] had questions about how one fell so thoroughly in battle against a floating eye. [[:silrie-aegiskiir | Silrie]] suggested, with the tact of someone who had seen the thunderwave, that the beholder had been a powerful opponent.

Back in the room of many doors, necrotic harm continued to press upon [[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]], [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]], and the assorted animals in their company. A trail in the disturbed dust showed comparatively recent passage between the [[The Wallarch District | Wallarch]] entrance, the door marked with [[The Eighth House | the defunct necromantic sigil]], and the door bearing the crest of [[House Oestra | House Oestra]].

[[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Deathwalker]] opened several doors by percussive means. Behind the necromancy sigil lay a colossal diorama:

bq). The passageway opens into an enormous space - the walls and ceiling almost lost in the haze of distance. The floor rises and falls in a stone simulacrum of rolling hills, you can even see the glint of the meagre light reflecting from the waves of what appears to be a river. You realise that you are surrounded by a motionless army of armed and armoured skeletons. Or rather, as you look closer, two armies frozen in the throws of a colossal conflict. A macabre diorama whose grandiose effect is spoiled only slightly by the fact that many of the skeletons are missing parts - the floor is littered with teeth and fingerbones that, presumably, were once part of the display. A clear path demarcated by bands of silver laid into the dark stone of the floor, wends its way between the posed skeletons.

With [[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]] needing respite, the [[The Adventurers | Wonch]] chose to leave by the [[House Oestra | Oestra]] door, following [[:razorbeak | their chicken]] guide into the deeper tunnels.

h3. Wandering in the Dark

They got lost.

Away from the chalked routes, the finished masonry of the archives gave way to rough natural rock. Yet the pattern persisted — pillared halls and bone alcoves repeated as though the _idea_ of a catacomb were propagating outward through the necromantic fallout, imposing itself on the living stone. A solemn procession of rats placed one of their dead in an alcove with ritual care, as if the architecture had taught even vermin proper burial.

Following a whisper of wind through the dark, the party found a tangle of tree roots and blood moss breaking through from above. They cleared the blockage to reveal a hidden door.

h3. The Shrine of the Queen of Ravens

Beyond lay a massacre around a makeshift shrine. Corpses in dark robes wore amulets bearing the symbol of the fallen necromantic house, but not the silver bracelets of legitimate Sindrelian service. They had died in agony, scattered across a small chapel draped in fabric that suggested wings engulfing the congregation. On the altar stood an idol of the Queen of Ravens in her aspect as vengeful reaper — obsidian and electrum, wings swept back, taloned hands outstretched.

Daubed on the wall in blood ancient blood: _AVENGE US._

The party looted what they could. [[:ayr | Ayr]] took an elaborate buckler from the lead corpse's wrist and found he could not remove it.

h3. Emergence

Another route led onward and upward. The tunnels spat the party out through the [[Osteomantic Archives | Osteomantic Archives]] of [[House Oestra | House Oestra]] — not the Wallarch branch they had entered, but one within the Leaguer itself.

[[:commander-locke | Prefect Locke]] marched over almost immediately, demanding to know how they had got in, why they had not followed his advice about weaponsmiths, and whether anything they intended to say could be considered the truth.

h3. Outwards

The further out they go, the less structured the tunnels and chambers become. The basic pattern of columned spaces and alcoves for bones is consistent, but the magically shaped stone of the inward regions gradually gives way to what look like natural formations. Here and there, smaller hollows in the walls are home to the skulls of rats and moles.

The necromantic radiation is pushing the pattern outwards, the _idea_ of the catacombs leaching out into the surrounding ground. Openings have appeared sporadically in the surrounding landscape, which is how Tama got it. These are unpredictable and the anathemists never found out about them.

If the players go far enough, they will find that the tunnels curve back in on themselves, leading them back towards the centre. If they have Tama with them, she can lead them to an intermittent opening near Fallowhurst. She can also warn them about the plague doctor.

h4. Rooms

h5. Hidden temple to the Queen of Ravens.

There was once a hidden door, but time has caused the mechanism to decay. The door is obvious, but getting it open is hard. A small cult developed within the necromancers. They were not part of Samhain's inner circle, so they died here. Each wears dark robes and an amulet bearing the symbol of house Samhain. They don't have the bracelets. They died in agony, their corpses are scattered around the room. 
 
The room is small by the scale of the catacombs, but large enough to contain a few makeshift pews and an alter to the Queen of Ravens. - A god of death. Dark fabric has been draped from the ceiling so as to suggest a pair of wings reaching out as if to engulf the congregation. On the alter is an idol - the Queen of Ravens in her aspect as the vengeful reaper - wings swept back in a predatory dive, talon-tipped hands outstretched. The idol is obsidian with electrum details. Removing it is an act of blasphemy against the Carrion Queen, and will have _consequences_.

The lead skeleton is wearing the remains of armour including an elaborate buckler on his left wrist. They are sprawled at the base of a wall, their outstretched hand at the end of a trail of dark stains that spell out Avenge Us.

h5. Halls of history
 
h6. The Seven Day War

This is the battle of the Planes of Herrman, 250 years ago. Deathwalker was dead for this but will recognise the uniforms of the Mage Lords of Houd and the Thaumocracy of Tellerin. The room has been laid out as a sort of timeline — the party enter where the Tellerite forces are fighting a desperate rearguard action. As they pass through they get the story in reverse: the Tellerite reinforcements arriving too late, the Houd defenders defeating the Tellerite expeditionary force, and finally, near the exit, the Battle of Brassmeade Bridge recreated in epic, propagandistic tones.

Some skeletons next to the path have a silver symbol inlaid on their skulls. Touching one brings an eerie light to the eyes as it recounts part of the battle:

* A fleeing tellerite: "Retreat! Sound the retreat!"
* A houdin soldier: "Tellerite reinforcements incoming! Looks like they're late to the party. Form ranks!"

There is room to cross the brass bridge between the giant's legs. Using it will destabilise the skeleton, causing the club to fall. Make a saving throw!
