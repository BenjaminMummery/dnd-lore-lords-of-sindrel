h2. The Crossroads

_Late afternoon of the 29th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

[[:ayr | Ayr]] had questions about how one fell so thoroughly in battle against a floating eye. [[:silrie-aegiskiir | Silrie]] suggested, with the tact of someone who had seen the thunderwave, that the beholder had been a powerful opponent.

Back in the room of many doors, necrotic harm continued to press upon [[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]], [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]], and the assorted animals in their company. A trail in the disturbed dust showed comparatively recent passage between the [[The Wallarch District | Wallarch]] entrance, the door marked with [[The Eighth House | the defunct necromantic sigil]], and the door bearing the crest of [[House Oestra | House Oestra]].

[[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Deathwalker]] opened several doors by percussive means. Behind the sigil of [[House Goela | House Goela]] lay a vaulted gallery of founders:

bq). The passageway opens into a long hall whose ceiling is lost in shadow. Eight tall niches line the walls, each holding a life-size statue carved from pale grey stone. Every figure wears the solemn grandeur of civic monument - founders posed for eternity, not for battle. What catches the light is silver: each statue's signature regalia has been rendered in metal so bright it seems almost liquid against the matte stone.
<br>
Nearest the door, a broad-shouldered figure in a craftsman's apron, sleeves rolled for work that will never finish. The hands are the marvel: gauntlets of silver so finely chased they might be gloves, fingers slightly curled as if shaping clay that is not there.
<br>
Next, a robed figure stands with hands folded beneath a deep hood. The face is a smooth mask of silver polished to a mirror finish - eyeless, inscrutable, reflecting only a distorted image of the observed.
<br>
Then a human warrior in full plate, chin lifted, visor raised to show a proud, hard face. His hands rest before him on the pommel of a silver greatsword so large it rises nearly to the height of his shoulder.
<br>
An elf in close-fitted robes with a high collar wears a circlet of silver at the brow. At the statue's feet, two lean hounds crouch in stone - jaws open, ribs showing - every tooth picked out in a darker vein of rock, their collars traced in silver thread.
<br>
A figure in a flowing robe stands with hands tucked into sleeves. An intricate filigree of silver has been inlaid into the stone robe - only the face is free of its tracery, and a steady drip of water running down the folds of the hood has weathered that beyond recognition.
<br>
A tiefling wears high trousers and a loose shirt open at the throat. Their hand rests loosely on the hilt of a shortsword at their side, hips canted in a louche swagger. An amulet of gleaming silver stars lies against the chest, each point sharp enough to cut.
<br>
A dwarf woman in heavy armour stands square to the room, hair cropped short above a stern, unyielding face. A shortsword rests at her side, notched with swordbreaker teeth along the back of the blade. Her hand rests atop a colossal tower shield almost as tall as she is, its face inlaid with silver knotwork.
<br>
The eighth niche is wrong. There is no statue - only a bare plinth, a drift of fine grey dust at its foot, and scorch-pitting on the rear wall as if something inside the stone had been unmade in an instant. A few flecks of silver glitter in the dust.

[[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Deathwalker]] took the silver greatsword from the Oestra figure's hands. It came away with a dull scrape; she wrapped cloth around the distinctive cross-guard and hilt until the house geometry was hidden. The blade had no edge worth speaking of and no balance a fighter would trust - cold ceremonial weight, useful only as loot or display.

Behind the necromancy sigil lay a colossal diorama:

bq). The passageway opens into an enormous space - the walls and ceiling almost lost in the haze of distance. The floor rises and falls in a stone simulacrum of rolling hills, you can even see the glint of the meagre light reflecting from the waves of what appears to be a river. You realise that you are surrounded by a motionless army of armed and armoured skeletons. Or rather, as you look closer, two armies frozen in the throes of a colossal conflict. A macabre diorama whose grandiose effect is spoiled only slightly by the fact that many of the skeletons are missing parts - the floor is littered with teeth and fingerbones that, presumably, were once part of the display. A clear path demarcated by bands of silver laid into the dark stone of the floor, wends its way between the posed skeletons.

With [[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]] needing respite, the [[The Adventurers | Wonch]] chose to leave by the [[House Oestra | Oestra]] door, following [[:razorbeak | their chicken]] guide into the deeper tunnels.

h2. Wandering in the Dark

_Late afternoon of the 29th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

They got lost.

Away from the chalked routes, the finished masonry of the archives gave way to rough natural rock. Yet the pattern persisted - pillared halls and bone alcoves repeated as though the _idea_ of a catacomb were propagating outward through the necromantic fallout, imposing itself on the living stone. A solemn procession of rats placed one of their dead in an alcove with ritual care, as if the architecture had taught even vermin proper burial.

Following a whisper of wind through the dark, the party found a tangle of tree roots and blood moss breaking through from above. They cleared the blockage to reveal a hidden door.

h2. The [[Shrine of the Queen of Ravens | Shrine of the Queen of Ravens]]

_Late afternoon of the 29th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

Beyond lay a massacre around a makeshift [[Shrine of the Queen of Ravens | shrine to the Queen of Ravens]]. Corpses in dark robes wore neck amulets bearing the sigil of [[The Eighth House | The Eighth House]]. They had died in agony, scattered across a small chapel draped in fabric that suggested wings engulfing the congregation. On the altar stood an idol of the [[The Gods | Queen of Ravens]] in her aspect as vengeful reaper - obsidian and electrum, wings swept back, taloned hands outstretched.

Daubed on the wall in ancient blood: _AVENGE US._

The party looted what they could. [[:ayr | Ayr]] took an elaborate buckler from the lead corpse's wrist and found he could not remove it.

h2. Emergence

_Evening of the 29th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

Another route led onward and upward. The tunnels spat the party out through the [[Osteomantic Archives | Osteomantic Archives]] of [[House Oestra | House Oestra]] - not the [[The Wallarch District | Wallarch]] branch they had entered, but one within the [[House Oestra | Leageur]] itself.

That alone was enough to unsettle anyone who believed the archives were merely local ossuaries. They had crossed the city underground without a map, a guide, or permission. The thought that the same bone-lined passages might reach *outward* - back toward the plains, the [[Arkay Mountain Range | Arkay]], [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]] - did not yet occur to them. It would, later, in the company of a much smaller witness.

[[:commander-locke | Prefect Locke]] marched over almost immediately, demanding to know how they had got in, why they had not followed his advice about weaponsmiths, and whether anything they intended to say could be considered the truth.
