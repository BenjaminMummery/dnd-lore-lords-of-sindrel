h3. The Crossroads

_Afternoon of the 29th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

[[:ayr | Ayr]] had questions about how one fell so thoroughly in battle against a floating eye. [[:silrie-aegiskiir | Silrie]] suggested, with the tact of someone who had seen the thunderwave, that the beholder had been a powerful opponent.

Back in the room of many doors, necrotic harm continued to press upon [[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]], [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]], and the assorted animals in their company. A trail in the disturbed dust showed comparatively recent passage between the [[The Wallarch District | Wallarch]] entrance, the door marked with [[The Eighth House | the defunct necromantic sigil]], and the door bearing the crest of [[House Oestra | House Oestra]].

[[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Deathwalker]] opened several doors by percussive means. Behind the necromancy sigil lay a colossal diorama:

bq). The passageway opens into an enormous space - the walls and ceiling almost lost in the haze of distance. The floor rises and falls in a stone simulacrum of rolling hills, you can even see the glint of the meagre light reflecting from the waves of what appears to be a river. You realise that you are surrounded by a motionless army of armed and armoured skeletons. Or rather, as you look closer, two armies frozen in the throws of a colossal conflict. A macabre diorama whose grandiose effect is spoiled only slightly by the fact that many of the skeletons are missing parts - the floor is littered with teeth and fingerbones that, presumably, were once part of the display. A clear path demarcated by bands of silver laid into the dark stone of the floor, wends its way between the posed skeletons.

With [[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]] needing respite, the [[The Adventurers | Wonch]] chose to leave by the [[House Oestra | Oestra]] door, following [[:razorbeak | their chicken]] guide into the deeper tunnels.

h3. Wandering in the Dark

They got lost.

Away from the chalked routes, the finished masonry of the archives gave way to rough natural rock. Yet the pattern persisted — pillared halls and bone alcoves repeated as though the _idea_ of a catacomb were propagating outward through the necromantic fallout, imposing itself on the living stone. A solemn procession of rats placed one of their dead in an alcove with ritual care, as if the architecture had taught even vermin proper burial.

Following a whisper of wind through the dark, the party found a tangle of tree roots and blood moss breaking through from above. They cleared the blockage to reveal a hidden door.

h3. The [[Shrine of the Queen of Ravens | Shrine of the Queen of Ravens]]

Beyond lay a massacre around a makeshift [[Shrine of the Queen of Ravens | shrine to the Queen of Ravens]]. Corpses in dark robes wore neck amulets bearing the sigil of [[The Eighth House | The Eighth House]]. They had died in agony, scattered across a small chapel draped in fabric that suggested wings engulfing the congregation. On the altar stood an idol of the [[The Gods | Queen of Ravens]] in her aspect as vengeful reaper — obsidian and electrum, wings swept back, taloned hands outstretched.

Daubed on the wall in ancient blood: _AVENGE US._

The party looted what they could. [[:ayr | Ayr]] took an elaborate buckler from the lead corpse's wrist and found he could not remove it.

h3. Emergence

Another route led onward and upward. The tunnels spat the party out through the [[Osteomantic Archives | Osteomantic Archives]] of [[House Oestra | House Oestra]] — not the [[The Wallarch District | Wallarch]] branch they had entered, but one within the [[House Oestra | Leageur]] itself.

[[:commander-locke | Prefect Locke]] marched over almost immediately, demanding to know how they had got in, why they had not followed his advice about weaponsmiths, and whether anything they intended to say could be considered the truth.
