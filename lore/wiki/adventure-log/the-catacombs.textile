h2. Heading Down

_Afternoon of the 29th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

bq). The stairs lead down into a vaulted space punctuated by stone pillars, the dry air growing colder as you descend. As in the rooms above, the walls are lined with skull-populated alcoves. You recognise the pattern from the main hall - the alcoves are densely populated near the stairs, growing more sparse further into the room. In the weak light filtering down behind you, you can make out passageways leading off from the other three walls.

The party's departure from the funeral of [[:micycle-smynth | Micycle Smynth]] lacked the dignity the occasion deserved. Robes were stolen, mortal remains were briefly airborne, and the officiants were avoided only by the narrowest of margins and a timely vision of the [[:embren-oestra | plague doctor]] that sent everyone running in the opposite direction.

They chose the passageway that led toward the [[Quarantine Zone | quarantine zone]].

h2. The Hall of Dragons

_Late afternoon of the 29th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

bq). The vault opens into a chamber built for scale. Above you, the articulated skeleton of a black dragon hangs from iron chains and calibrated counterweights, tail curled, wings spread in a posture of arrested flight. A narrow gallery rings the upper wall - bone railings, skull finials - as if scholars once observed the specimen from height. Below, on stepped plinths, smaller draconic remains lie arranged by lineage and size: wyrmling ribs no longer than your arm beside jawbones that could cradle a cart. Plaques name each display giving both an identity and a specialty. The colossal skull dominates the room; in its hollow cranium something shifts - a scraping of claw on bone, too small for the beast that once owned it.

This was [[:kithrak-the-black | Kithrak the Black]] - or what remained of him.

Movement came from the skull. A miniature black dragon, nesting inside the colossal one, scrambled down to investigate the intruders. The party adopted [[:kithrak-ii | Kithrak II]], slayer of mice, on the spot.

The deeper they went, the stronger the necromantic presence became - a slow, ambient harm that seemed to reanimate stored remains in inverse proportion to their size. [[:kithrak-ii | Kithrak II]], being the smallest, was the liveliest. [[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]] and [[:silrie-aegiskiir | Silrie]], reading the labels and arrangements, pieced together the archives' original purpose: in a society where _Speak with Dead_ was commonplace, the dead themselves were a library. Each skeleton was an expert waiting to be consulted.

White chalk marked a path through the tunnels. [[:pin | Pin]], seeing an opportunity to confound an enemy, rubbed the marks away.

h2. The Hall of Sages

_Late afternoon of the 29th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

bq). A circular seminar hall, or something close to it: a dozen high-backed chairs arranged in a loose crescent, each occupied by a skeleton in the remains of scholar's robes. Before every seat stands a lectern of dark wood banded in silver; the papers are long since rotted away, the ink wells dry as dust. One figure gestures mid-argument, finger bone extended; another rests chin on knotted hands; a third has slumped as if asleep at the height of a dull lecture. The arrangement is too purposeful for mass burial - this is a faculty meeting stopped in amber.

[[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]] acquired a fresh set of archive robes. Nobody touched the lecterns. Some lessons are learned the hard way; others are simply observed and filed under "later."

h2. The Crossroads

_Late afternoon of the 29th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

The passageways opened into a nonagonal chamber with nine doors. Each bore a house sigil save the entrance they had used. Two doors were chalked: one marked [[House Oestra | House Oestra]], the other the sigil of [[The Eighth House | the defunct necromantic house]].

h2. The Hall of Aberrations

_Late afternoon of the 29th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

bq). The air changes before the architecture explains why - a faint pressure at the temples, the sense of being observed from directions geometry does not admit. The chamber is a taxidermist's nightmare rendered in bone: bulbous craniums and too many limbs suspended on wire and bracket, labels written in scripts that hurt to read sidelong. An illithid hangs splayed like a student's nightmare made permanent. An otyugh's rib cage is grotesquely ornate. Slaad bones are chalked in colours the pigment has long lost. At the centre, tethered by brass chains to a ring in the ceiling, a beholder skeleton hangs - the whole array glowing faintly with a necromantic residue that has outlived the flesh.

[[:ayr | Ayr]] woke [[:zalatan | the beholder]] with an ill-considered stretch. Combat followed. [[:ayr | Ayr]] landed a solid blow before [[:amanira | Amanira]]'s thunderwave caught friend and foe alike; [[:ayr | Ayr]] failed to dodge his own party and dropped into unconsciousness with unfortunate thoroughness.

[[:rossin-ros-greyhirst | Ros]] could not negotiate peace with an undead eye tyrant. [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Deathwalker]], [[:pin | Pin]], and [[:wilrin-racenglade | Wilrin]] finished what remained of [[:zalatan | Zalatan]]. The party took rubbings of the sigils on the walls and retreated toward the surface, the necrotic weight of the depths pressing harder with every step they took inward.
