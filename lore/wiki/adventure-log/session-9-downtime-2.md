_Evening of the 29th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

Questioned by [[:commander-locke | Prefect Locke]], the gang concoct an acceptable, if not entirely believable, lie, claiming to have wandered down into the catacombs beneath an unspecified Osteomantic Archive at random.

With Locke seemingly perturbed at our not having followed his suggestion of investigating local weaponsmiths for any connection to Anathemist activity, we seize the chance to do a stock take of House Oestra’s wares. Nothing seems to be amiss. 

The gang are directed towards their new base of operations, a town house in Upper Sindril: Radwasp House. It has all we could wish for and more, it seems, as well as an amenable butler named Cedric. 

_The 30th of Enean, year 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]])_

After a much needed rest, the gang opt for a quieter day of reflection and downtime. Before heading out, [[:journeyman-galoras | Journeyman Galoras]] pays a visit to deliver Ayr’s new weapon, a force multiplying sword which gathers a single charge each day, potentially able to release untold power.

Various actions are taken about town:
* Ros seeks materials for a new weapon, some kind of chain and spearhead contraption to be constructed by Galoras
* Silrie seeks out a pair of magical knuckle dusters (are these also to be made by Galoras?) then visits the temple of the Dawn Father and receives an ominous vision warning of dangers on the road ahead
* Amanira seeks out various books to dig further into the history of the city
* DW and Wilrin go clothes shopping
* Ayr, always hunting for newer and better gear, meets with Kelferria the Smith and commissions a potentially v expensive set of plate armour. He also learns that they have been overworked with some sort of LARGE ORDER. Might be worth following up on

Back at Radwasp House, Cedric has constructed a like-for-like replica of the building in miniature form for the chicken to live in and we learn that the cat, who had seemed out of sorts earlier in the day, has gone. 

A letter has arrived for Wilrin. He has been provisionally accepted as a student of [[The Heptade Academy | The Heptade Academy]], on completion of our current activities and investigations. Using the magical super secure training room in Radwasp house and spurred on by the good news, Wilrin spends the evening practicing his magical skills, and gains some understanding of the wild energies coursing through him.

Heading to bed, Ayr attempts to remove the buckler he found in the shrine to the Lady of Ravens, but finds he cannot. He dreams he is a winged beast, streaking through the skies above Sindril, seeing the roads and streets below as rivers of blood - a product of the Noble class continually trodding down on the everyday denizens of the city. He is drawn to the vital fluids pumping through the veins of the rich and well-to-do residents of the Upper City.

h3. Amanira's Books

* Most of the books about Sindrel are written by and for Sindrelians, and tell the same story of the city's founding - 7 great mages created a utopia where magic could be studied without interference or restriction. Each founded a house to study their particular form of magic.
* The more esoteric tomes mention an eighth mage - the necromancer - who turned against the others. It took the combined strength of all seven to defeat the traitor, and the cost was great. Accounts differ as to whether they were slain, wounded, or simply dismayed by the experience, but they stepped away from the octrarion circle, handing the rule of the city over to their successors.
* One book differs - The Rise and Fall of Houd is a dense historical treatise on the Magedom of Houd, the empire that once united the northern continent. Some of the contemporary documents it quotes make reference to a magical disturbance in the far north, and one refers to a party of investigators being dispatched from the Soulwind Institute.
* One particularly odd book has a combination lock on the spine, but doesn't appear to lock. It'll require. A very high wisdom score is required to figure this one out.

Navinea is genuine about her request, but also looking to keep tabs on the investigation.
Sindrel's situation is more dire than they're letting on. Grimbolg have been stepping up production to meet demand, but they're struggling against entropy. The garbage collection system has had to expand rapidly, and the has grown increasingly disorganised (thank the cats for that). The sabotage to the water system that killed [[:dogara | D'gara]] also ruined a large quantity of stored Nitre - a preservative and fertiliser that Grimbolg use to both supplement and store food, making their situation even more precipitous.
