For the vast majority of world history, magic has been the dominant form of power. The world has been shaped around it, with magical strength becoming the primary shaping force of politics. Over the last few centuries, however, it has become undeniable that magic is dying out. As the previously iron-clad power structures sought to shore up their suddenly crumbling positions, wars and revolutions become increasingly common.

The northern regions of the continent of [[Artresh | Artresh]] are a patchwork of city states and petty kingdoms, the fractured remains of the once semi-unified [[The Magedom of Houd | Magedom of Houd]]. Following the Seven-Day War with the [[The Thaumocracy of Tellerin | Tellerites]], the Mage Lord Council governing the Magedom, never the most cohesive group at the best of times, fractured. The resulting states are embroiled in an ever-shifting network of alliance, feud, political marriage, and, occasionally, actual war. [[Soulwind | Soulwind]], on the northern coast, is the largest of these states by area, although its territory consists mostly of inhospitable mountainous land with little in the way of resources.

To the south is the mysterious [[The Thaumocracy of Tellerin | Thaumocracy of Tellerin]], a once-great and expansionist empire. After their unsuccessful attempt to invade Houd, the empire retreated, closing its borders to outsiders.

h2. *Historical Events*

_Dates use the [[The Soulwind Calendar | Soulwind calendar (SC)]]. Current year: **4218 SC** (adventure log canon). “Years ago” figures are measured from 4218._

[accordion]
    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~3010 SC (_~1208 years ago_) — [[The Magedom of Houd | The Magedom of Houd]] [end-title]
        [content]
            * The northern kingdoms of [[Artresh | Artresh]] unite under the Mage Lord Council as the [[The Magedom of Houd | Magedom of Houd]] — well established by the time the rift is discovered two centuries later.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~3180 SC (_~1038 years ago_) — [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]] and [[House Oestra | House Oestra]] [end-title]
        [content]
            * Long before the rift: [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]] exists as a fishing village on a shallow ford of the River Deren.
            * [[House Oestra | House Oestra]] establishes a family estate — manor, bridge toll, and levees to drain land for the build. The village predates everything that follows.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] 3218 SC (_~1000 years ago_) — Founding of [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] [end-title]
        [content]
            * A dimensional tear is discovered in the far north. The Soulwind Institute Dimensional Research Group (SinDReG) is dispatched to investigate.
            * They establish a semi-permanent camp — the Soulwind Institute Dimensional Research Laboratory ([[Sindrel | SinDReL]]) — around the rift. This camp is the founding of [[Sindrel | Sindrel]].
            * Early forays through the tear prove other worlds exist, but the rift is too unstable for sustained use.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~3220s SC (_~990s years ago_) — Rift control and growth [end-title]
        [content]
            * The Circle plans **rift stabilisation** — a project to bring the tear under direct control.
            * Small numbers of additional mages and supplies arrive; the camp begins to grow into a city.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~3226 SC (_~992 years ago_) — [[The Mage Road | Soulwind conduit]] [end-title]
        [content]
            * [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] persuades [[The Soulwind Institute | the Soulwind Institute]] to link [[Soulwind | Soulwind]] and the city with a **two-way** magical conduit along [[The Mage Road | the Mage Road]] — pitched as mutual aid, in practice the backbone of Sindrel's growing power.
            * Transference towers dot the route. Once stabilisation is complete, the conduit will also supply short, intense bursts of power to refocus the rift between targets.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~3228 SC (_~990 years ago_) — Rift stabilisation [end-title]
        [content]
            * Stabilisation succeeds. The rift is under Circle control: it can only stay open for short periods, but long enough for earnest exploration.
            * The conduit operates as promised — two-way travel and trade with [[Soulwind | Soulwind]], plus burst refocusing when a new world must be targeted.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~3230s SC (_~980s years ago_) — Exploration and the Great Houses [end-title]
        [content]
            * Earnest exploration of the linked worlds. [[Zotikó | Zotikó]] (Vitality): the [[Peaches of Immortality | peaches]] are discovered, guarded, and not accessible without bloodshed. [[Thanismot | Thanismot]]: [[:mistress-lucretia-samhain | Samhain]] finds it nullifies her vampiric thirst and sunlight vulnerability. [[Kosmios | Kosmios]]: a goldmine of resources, functionally defenceless against magic. Brief forays to other worlds; gods and demons are too dangerous to tangle with routinely.
            * Wealth from [[Kosmios | Kosmios]] allows [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] to support itself and grow independent of [[Soulwind | Soulwind]], while keeping close ties and the conduit.
            * The Great Houses are established as training institutions for arriving mages.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~3234 SC (_~984 years ago_) — [[Fallowhurst College | Fallowhurst College]] [end-title]
        [content]
            * [[:lord-ablas-oestra | Lord Oestra]] moves the seat of [[House Oestra | House Oestra]] from [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]] to [[Sindrel | Sindrel]].
            * [[:embren-oestra | Embren Oestra]] establishes [[Fallowhurst College | Fallowhurst College]] at the family estate to study healing magic.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~3238 SC (_~980 years ago_) — Calamity at [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]] [end-title]
        [content]
            * Founders' ambition grows; the Circle debates **Project Riftlock** — permanently targeting the rift to [[Kosmios | Kosmios]] for extractive colonisation. [[:mistress-lucretia-samhain | Samhain]] dissents.
            * [[:lord-ablas-oestra | Lord Oestra]], still mortal and desperate for immortality, pushes the college toward dangerous experimentation. See [[Letter from Lord Oestra to Embren Oestra | Lord Oestra's letter]] (14 Penderus 3238).
            * The college suffers a magical disaster.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~3242 SC (_~976 years ago_) — Peach theft and founder immortality [end-title]
        [content]
            * An early test of enhanced rift control — prototyping for **Riftlock** — lets the founders **steal** the [[Peaches of Immortality | peaches]] from [[Zotikó | Zotikó]], leaving devastation behind.
            * **Six** founders eat the fruit. [[:first-of-mabon | First of Mabon]] achieves immortality through peach-based artifice ([[The Amulet of Stars | The Amulet of Stars]]) instead of eating.
            * [[:mistress-lucretia-samhain | Samhain]] refuses and reveals her vampirism. Distrust grows.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~3245 SC (_~973 years ago_) — Riftlock and [[The Fall of the Eighth House | the Fall of the Eighth House]] [end-title]
        [content]
            * **Riftlock** is executed: the rift is locked toward [[Kosmios | Kosmios]]. The seven conspire to trap [[:mistress-lucretia-samhain | Samhain]]; she outmaneuvers them, but is ultimately imprisoned in [[Thanismot | Thanismot]] when the lock closes the Mortality gate behind her.
            * The huge initial power draw **destroys the Soulwind conduit**. Only the [[The Mage Road | Mage Road]] tower ruins remain.
            * Her deadwoman switch kills every necromancer still in [[Sindrel | Sindrel]]. [[:lord-ablas-oestra | Lord Oestra]] curses her likeness from the record. See [[The Fall of the Eighth House | The Fall of the Eighth House]].
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~3288 SC (_~930 years ago_) — [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]] abandoned [end-title]
        [content]
            * ~fifty years after the college calamity, the last villagers leave. The bound scholars in the operating theatre continue to abduct **travellers** along [[The Mage Road | the Mage Road]] — the fresher corpses mistaken for recent village dead.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~3298 SC (_~920 years ago_) — The Drowned Crossing [end-title]
        [content]
            * Within a decade of abandonment, the levees fail. The Deren floods [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]] to a depth of several feet — the estate survives on raised foundations; the village does not.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~3250–3800 SC — Kosmios trade empire [end-title]
        [content]
            * [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] thrives as the heart of an extractive trade empire. Colonial wealth flows through the locked rift.
            * The outpouring of magic from [[Arcan | Arcan]] into [[Kosmios | Kosmios]] begins to destabilise the Wheel of Forms — the long decline known as the death of magic.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~3850 SC (_~368 years ago_) — Death of magic [end-title]
        [content]
            * The first stirrings of the death of magic are felt across [[Arcan | Arcan]].
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~3900 SC (_~318 years ago_) — [[Soulwind | Soulwind]]'s decline [end-title]
        [content]
            * As magic fades, [[Soulwind | Soulwind]]'s population diminishes. [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] becomes the centre of magical power on [[Arcan | Arcan]].
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] 3958 SC (_260 years ago_) — The Seven Day War [end-title]
        [content]
            * Scarcity of magical resources drives conflict between [[The Magedom of Houd | The Magedom of Houd]] and [[The Thaumocracy of Tellerin | The Thaumocracy of Tellerin]]. The Tellerites are generally remembered as the aggressors; the Houdis were ultimately victorious.
            * Decisive fighting on the Planes of Herrman. Tellerite advance delayed at the Battle of Brassmeade Bridge — [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Nakoma Deathwalker Mor'got'ha]] and a handful of goliath warriors hold the crossing for **ten days** before the bridge is destroyed; she is slain and later resurrected by [[:nitherfang-gilfrarrin | Nitherfang Gilfrarrin]].
            * Defeated Tellerites retreat and close their borders. The weakened Magedom gradually disintegrates into northern city-states.
            * Over the following decades, contractor networks begin delivering war dead to the [[Osteomantic Archives | Osteomantic Archives]]; the **Seven Day War** diorama is installed piecemeal and expanded across the **3960s–4020s SC**.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~4185 SC (_~33 years ago_) — The [[Anathemists | Anathemists]] [end-title]
        [content]
            * Inter-faith cells emerge in the north, preaching that hubristic magic caused the gods to withdraw. For decades they remain fringe.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~4213 SC (_~5 years ago_) — [[Anathemists | Anathemist]] escalation [end-title]
        [content]
            * As magic fades faster, [[Anathemists | Anathemist]] cells grow bolder — probing [[The Mage Road | the Mage Road]], openly hostile to traffic bound for [[Sindrel | Sindrel]]. Infiltration of the city accelerates in the run-up to **4218 SC**.
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] 4217 SC (_~1 year ago_) — The Mad King of [[Corvus | Corvus]] [end-title]
        [content]
            * In the city-state of **Corvus** — one of the petty kingdoms fractured from [[The Magedom of Houd | Houd]] after the Seven Day War — a tyrant known as the Mad King hires mercenaries (including the Black Bloods) to crush a rebel uprising.
            * The rebels devastate the royal army; [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Nakoma Deathwalker Mor'got'ha]] fights on the rebel side. [[:pin | Pin]] (~**21**) and the Black Bloods fight for the King; [[:pin | Pin]]'s friend Essen is executed on the field. See [[Hesh | Hesh]].
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

    [accordion-item]
        [title] ~4150 SC (_~68 years ago_) — [[Project Kosmios | Project Kosmios]] [end-title]
        [content]
            * Realising their position on a fading [[Arcan | Arcan]] is unsustainable, the founders plan to move the entire city of [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] into [[Kosmios | Kosmios]].
        [end-content]
    [end-accordion-item]

h2. GM notes

* **Anchor year:** 4218 SC (31st Enean) — all “years ago” on this page are from 4218.
* **Founders:** Six peach-eaters and [[:first-of-mabon | First of Mabon]] (peach-based artifice) survive; immortality dates to ~3242 SC (founding generation). Samhain imprisoned in [[Thanismot | Thanismot]] since ~3245 SC (Riftlock).
* **Conduit:** Destroyed at Riftlock (~3245 SC); tower ruins remain on [[The Mage Road | the Mage Road]].
* **3958:** Seven Day War (~260 years before 4218). Archives war diorama grows **3960s–4020s** via contractor dead-delivery networks.
* **~4217:** Mad King of **Corvus** — [[:pin | Pin]] (~21) and [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Deathwalker]] on opposite sides.
* **~4185 / ~4213:** [[Anathemists | Anathemists]] emerge (~33 years ago); escalate sharply ~5 years before present.
* **~4150:** [[Project Kosmios | Project Kosmios]] — move [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] bodily into [[Kosmios | Kosmios]]; see [[The Octarion Circle | Octarion Circle]] for founder afflictions and the seven-house limitation.
