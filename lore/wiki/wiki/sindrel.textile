_The Last City of Magic._

bq). Sindrel sits on a high promontory jutting far out over the [[Norward Sea | Norward Sea]]. Even the city’s location is impossible – the barren, mountainous ground supports no farmland that could feed it, nor is there anything resembling a source of fresh water here. But that is not what captures your eye nor fires your imagination. Banners bearing the device of an eight pointed star hang from high white walls, above which rise a majestic profusion of spires and plazas. The city is clearly, ostentatiously magical. In a world where the smallest illusion can be the talk of the town, Sindrel’s use of magical power is breathtakingly profligate. Above the walls, rising spires become lost in the structures that hang down from above – buildings that float impossibly in the air, constructed to mirror themselves vertically in a way that defies any idea of terrestrial construction. Between and above them pass airships, great vessels with gilded prows and shimmering sails that glide effortlessly through open air with no visible means of support.
<br>
Through the centre of the city rises a narrow, white spire - a tower over a mile tall. Ringing its point like a halo is a colossal circle of stone: a floating street studded with docks and warehouses serving the fleet of airships.

Sindrel is, so far as anyone knows, the last place on [[Arcan | Arcan]] where the death of magic has not taken hold. Why this is or how long this state of affairs will last is unclear. Sindrel has, naturally, become immensely wealthy and powerful off the back of trading in magical goods and services. It has also become a mixing pot and haven for all those seeking to pursue magic.

h2. Races of Sindrel

Sindrel's ancestry mix is *not* a single citywide average. Three populations overlap everywhere, but each dominates different streets and social levels. Continental baseline for outsiders: [[Artresh | Artresh]] (*Races of Artresh*).

h3. Three populations

h4. 1. Native Sindrellians

Families and lines that have lived in the city for generations - house retainers, archive dynasties, old Wallarch trades, born citizens. Shielded from the death of magic, their makeup reflects *[[Artresh | Artresh]] before the Fade*: elves, tieflings, aasimar, and other magically inclined ancestries are unremarkable among established Sindrellian families. Wealth ranges from house Factors to honest craftspeople, but not the "just arrived with a caravan" poor. Densest in house precincts and older institutions.

h4. 2. Visiting dignitaries and traders

Ambassadors, merchant princes, pilgrimage sponsors, and anyone who can *afford* the journey to Sindrel. They arrive for business, spectacle, or politics and often leave again. Typically wealthy; poor people do not make the trip. Most visible in the upper city, house salons, airship terraces, and the [[Merchant's Quarter | Merchant's Quarter]]. They look like *current* [[Artresh | Artresh]] - mundane ancestries dominant. A tiefling ambassador is a talking point; a human one is not.

h4. 3. Refugees and the magically dispossessed

Those who *cannot* make a life elsewhere as magic dies - tieflings, aasimar, genasi, fey-touched peoples, and others whose existence depends on ambient magic. Typically impoverished: last savings, house charity, or the informal economy. Clustered in lower tiers, [[The Wallarch District | Wallarch]], cheap [[Dockward | Dockward]] lodgings, and the edges of the [[Quarantine Zone | Quarantine Zone]]. Magically sustained ancestries are *over-represented* here even compared to native Sindrellians.

h2. Geography

Situated among the inhospitable [[Arkay Mountain Range | Arkay Mountains]], Sindrel is a remote destination for almost any traveller, and reaching it is no trivial feat. The nearest conurbation of any note is [[Soulwind | Soulwind]], 130 leagues to the South-West.

h2. History

Sindrel was founded some time in the 3200s [[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]], approximately 1000 ago. As such, its early days are remembered primarily as a myth.

The story goes that the eight greatest mages of their day, each an unassailable master of their chosen school of magic, founded the city as a haven of magical study, free from the petty politics of the surrounding kingdoms. They formed the first Octarion Circle, and ruled over a period of peace and prosperity. But their golden age was not to last - one of their number, the great necromancer, grew jealous of the rest and plotted their downfall. In a dark ritual they sacrificed every one of their followers, ascending to something approaching godhood. Faced with this necromantic apotheosis, the other seven mages and their great houses of magic formed a desperate alliance. It took all of their power, but they were ultimately able to defeat the new god of death and save the world.

While the original seven are, of course, long gone, their legacy and names live on in the Great Houses that now rule Sindrel.

h2. Religion

Sindrel has long treated religion as tolerated eccentricity: useful for funerals, private comfort, and old family rites, but faintly embarrassing in serious civic life. Since the [[Anathemists | Anathemist]] threat became public, explicitly religious practice has become politically suspect, because many Sindrelians conflate devotion, anti-magical sentiment, and Anathemist fanaticism.

As it grew out of the culture of [[The Magedom of Houd | The Magedom of Houd]], [[The Gods | The Houdian Panthion]] is the main object of religious observance.

h2. Economics

For most of its history, Sindrel has competed on a roughy even footing with other centres of magical learning, most notably its closest neighbour [[Soulwind | Soulwind]]. In recent centuries, however, the death of magic has given the city an almost unopposed monopoly on magical goods and services. The city has become the hub of a trade empire that spans most of the northern continent, and is rumoured to extend even further south into the mysterious [[The Thaumocracy of Tellerin | Thaumocracy of Tellerin]].

h2. Politics

Sindrel is ruled by the seven Great Houses - the legacy and, in some cases, literal scions of  the original founding mages. Each house specialise in one school of magic, and fulfil a unique role within the operation of the city.

Ultimate authority is vested in the Octarion Council. Comprising the heads of each of the houses, the council boasts an unbroken history dating back to the original Octarion Council. The membership may have changed over time, but the _organisation_ has never faltered.

Each house has its own independent and often byzantine hierarchy, and it is via these chains of command that the decisions of the house heads are disseminated and ultimately enacted. At time, issues arise that require intermural cooperation but don't rise to the level of requiring the attention of house heads. In these cases, sub-councils are formed. Like the Octarion Council these comprise representatives of each house, albeit of a lesser rank. These are named numerically depending on the approximate rank of their membership, so a Hexine Council may convene to address an issue that falls beyond the reach of a Pential Council, but does not require the intervention of the luminaries who would make up a Septural Council.

Being limited in both space and available resources, Sindrel operates tight population controls. Visitors to the city may remain for a maximum of one month (36 days), and may not return for another month. Those wishing to become permanent residents require the patronage of one of the Houses.

Individuals in receipt of such patronage are known as _Adjutants_. Where it is necessary to differentiate, full members of the Houses are known as _Factors_, although it is more common simply to speak of such people as being 'of' their house. For example, [[:hielaman | Captain Hielaman]] would be referred to as being a Factor of [[House Meness | House Meness]], but it would be more common to refer to him as " [[:hielaman | Captain Hielaman]] of [[House Meness | House Meness]]."

Patronage is displayed on the person. [[Adjutant | Adjutants]] wear gold bracelets bearing their house's mark. [[Factor | Factors]] bear an enchanted tattoo of their house sigil - often on the hand or forearm, where it can be shown at need. Visitors without patronage receive silver bracelets for the duration of their stay; raised, these display a map and the number of days remaining.

This system has created a de-facto class structure with the Houses in the role of aristocracy and their adjutants as proletariat.

h2. Districts

Though often described as a single city, Sindrel is more accurately understood as two overlapping cities occupying the same space. The Lower City rests upon the ground beneath the floating districts, spreading outward from the base of [[The Fulcrum | the Fulcrum]]. Here are found the markets, warehouses, academies, military installations, and residential districts that sustain daily life. Many Sindrellans, particularly [[Adjutant | Adjutants]], spend the majority of their lives in the Lower City, traversing its streets and climbing the great ramps that connect it to the levels above.

Suspended overhead is the Upper City, a network of floating plazas, bridges, towers, and estates connected by an intricate lattice of walkways. Airships pass between these structures like gondolas through a crowded harbour, while merchants, diplomats, and nobles conduct their affairs high above the streets below. To visitors arriving from abroad, the Upper City is often the face of Sindrel; a dazzling display of magical architecture that seems to defy gravity itself.

The two halves of the city are linked by a series of broad ascending ramps that spiral gradually upward from the Lower City to the floating districts. Most citizens make these journeys on foot. The wealthy travel by carriages drawn by summoned beasts, while the most influential members of the Great Houses make use of [[House Beltus | House Beltus]]'s teleportation network to bypass distance altogether.

At the centre of both cities stands [[The Fulcrum | The Fulcrum]]. Rising from the ground and extending through the floating districts to heights beyond [[Dockward | Dockward]], it serves as the political heart of Sindrel and the most visible reminder that all parts of the city ultimately answer to the same authority.

h3. Lower City Districts

[[Merchant's Quarter | Merchant's Quarter]] - The principal point of arrival for overland travellers entering the city by way of [[The Mage Road | the Mage Road.]]
[[Granary Ward | Granary Ward]] - The logistical heart of the city, centred on the Amalthean (colloquially the Cornucopia) and responsible for distributing food and supplies throughout Sindrel.
[[Sevenways | Sevenways]] - A sprawling residential district built around an ancient network of ramps and bridges connecting the city's various levels.
[[The Wallarch District | Wallarch]] - Sindrel's poorest district, pressed between the city's outer wall and the [[Quarantine Zone | Quarantine Zone]].
[[Quarantine Zone | Quarantine Zone]] - The sealed ruins of [[The Eighth House | The Eighth House]], contaminated by dangerous necromantic energies since the fall of the Eighth.
The [[House Oestra | Leageur]] - The fortified military enclave of [[House Oestra | House Oestra]].

h3. Upper City Districts

[[Merrylane | Merrylane]] - A district of embassies, parks, and elegant residences favoured by diplomats and visiting dignitaries.
[[Scholars Ward | Scholars Ward]] - Home to [[The Heptade Academy | the Heptade Academy]] and much of Sindrel's academic and magical elite. Students, professors, researchers, and House scholars crowd its bridges, libraries, and lecture halls.
[[Dockward | Dockward]] - The centre of Sindrel's airship trade, a mile-wide floating ring suspended around the upper reaches of [[The Fulcrum | the Fulcrum]] like a colossal halo. Colloquially called _the annulus_, it is the gateway through which much of the city's commerce enters and leaves.

h3. Singular Landmarks

Some locations exist outside the ordinary district structure.

[[The Fulcrum | The Fulcrum]] - The political and symbolic heart of Sindrel.
The Panopticon - The floating headquarters of [[House Meness | House Meness]], which continuously circles the city.

h3. Races of Sindrel — NPC generation

When generating or placing an NPC, decide *which population they belong to first* - then pick species from that profile.

*Species profile (native tiers):*

* *Core:* human, elf, half-elf, dwarf, gnome, halfling
* *Common:* tiefling, aasimar, dragonborn, genasi, half-orc
* *Uncommon:* tabaxi, kenku, triton, changeling, shifter, duergar, drow, deep gnome
* *Rare:* fairy, satyr, eladrin, firbolg, harengon
* *Restricted:* same as [[Artresh | Artresh]] (*Non-existent* and plot-only kinds)

*Species profile (visitor tiers):* follow current [[Artresh | Artresh]] tiers (*Races of Artresh*).

*Species profile (refugee tiers — higher magical proportion than natives):*

* *Core:* tiefling, aasimar, genasi, half-elf, elf
* *Common:* changeling, shifter, dragonborn, fairy, satyr, eladrin, firbolg, gnome
* *Uncommon:* human, dwarf, halfling
* *Rare:* orc, half-orc, goblin
* *Restricted:* same as [[Artresh | Artresh]]

*Restricted (all populations):* monstrous ancestries, planar entities in mortal guise, undead peoples, and off-world kinds - plot-only unless explicitly introduced.

Founder statues and house art are *flavour*, not quotas. A room full of Lithran courtiers does not require eladrin.

h4. By district (which population dominates)

* *Upper city and house precincts* - **visitors** in public rooms; **native** staff behind the scenes. Refugees as petitioners or labour - not the default guest.
* *[[Merchant's Quarter | Merchant's Quarter]] and airship terraces* - **visitors** arriving and departing; **native** clerks running the counting-houses.
* *[[Dockward | Dockward]]* - all three: **refugee** dock labour; **native** longshoremen; **visitor** cargo owners.
* *[[The Fulcrum | Fulcrum]] and [[Merrylane | Merrylane]]* - **native** baseline; **visitor** Hexine Circle delegates; occasional **refugee** talent off the books.
* *[[The Wallarch District | Wallarch]]* - **refugee** and **native** working poor; mundane **visitor** traders on business only.
* *[[Quarantine Zone | Quarantine Zone]]* - do not treat as a species pool; plot-driven only.

h3. The Stay Ledger

Public belief: silver bracelets are visitor passes (map, countdown, stay limit); gold marks [[Adjutant | Adjutants]]; tattoos mark [[Factor | Factors]]. Nothing more.

GM truth: the **Office of Visitation** (Fulcrum bureaucracy, [[House Grimbolg | Grimbolg]] forge, [[House Meness | Meness]] gate enforcement) maintains a secret **Stay Ledger** - serial, true name, entry gate, and timestamp for every silver binding. Ward-stones under the city log passage when a bound bracelet crosses a district threshold. Gold reports to patron house only; Factor tattoos are not on the guest ledger.

**Binding ritual** (not explained to visitors): spoken name on the manifest; putting on the band; brief contact with an attuned gold bracelet or Factor tattoo at the inner gate. The Wonch completed all of this at arrival without recognising it as magic.

Ledger access: gate clerks (partial), Meness intelligence, Lithra identity office, Goela audit seers, and [[The Black Cats | Black Cat]] **Vault** leadership (via stolen extracts). Not general adjutants or the public.

h3. [[The Eighth House | House Samhain]], the dark lords of Necromancy,

Leader: [[:mistress-lucretia-samhain | Mistress Lucretia Samhain]]

Like [[House Oestra | House Oestra]], [[The Eighth House | House Samhain]] was a noble house of [[Soulwind | Soulwind]]. Unlike [[House Oestra | House Oestra]], the scions of [[The Eighth House | Samhain]] were, in fact, vampiric necromancers.

Unlike the other members of the octet, [[:mistress-lucretia-samhain | Mistress Lucretia Samhain]] did not partake of the peaches of immortality - being undead she was concerned that their effect on her might not be quite so positive. Having been effectively immortal for some time, she also had a somewhat different perspective than the rest of her compatriots. Where they grew ambitious and covetous, she was already jaded. During the early exploration, Samhain realised that the realm of [[Thanismot | Thanismot]] was something of a paradise for vampires - the sunlight did not burn her skin and the endless thirst for blood was absent as the deathly energies of the realm sustained her. She began spending more and more time there, ultimately moving her entire house into the realm full time. From that base, she began actively recruiting necromancers. Never a popular bunch, a majority were more than happy to accept her offer, especially once they had tasted the power of [[Thanismot | Mortality]].

When the rest of the Octet decided to focus on exploiting Mundanity, Samhain was the sole dissenting voice. In secret the rest of the council conspired to seal the entry to [[Thanismot | Mortality]], trapping her and her house in that realm.

An initial but brief period of infighting followed as the more ambitious or evil necromancers returned to their old ways, vying for power.

Over the next 500 years, the members of [[The Eighth House | house Samhain]] abandoned the great hall they had originally built around the portal and drifted into small scattered communities. Surrounded by death, their necromantic abilities allowed them to live safely and comfortably, and they have turned their abilities towards grand works of the magical art. As a result, the landscape of [[Thanismot | Mortality]] is adorned with impossible architecture, majestic statuary, and elegant gardens of undead plants.

* [[The Eighth House | House Samhain]] - the necromancers. They originally maintained a great archive of the dead, using the ability to summon shades of the past as an information retrieval system. Over time, more and more of them took up residence in the [[Thanismot | realm of death]], and ultimately Samhain was betrayed by the other houses and imprisoned. The practice of placing the bones of the dead in the great ossuaries persists, but it serves no practical purpose.

h3. Politics

While the houses are broadly in alignment, there is always internal strife.

h4. Alliances

* *Grimbolg + Mabon* - The transmuters and enchanters are both craftspeople and creators.
* *Oestra + Meness* - The evokers and abjurers are both martial and frequently operate in concert.
* *Lithra + Goela* - The illusionists and diviners both work in information and control.

h4. Rivalries

* *Lithra and Goela vs Meness and Oestra* - The illusionists and diviners are the embodiment of soft power. They find the martial approach distasteful.
* *Beltus x Mabon and Grimbolg* - The craftspeople value working with their hands, and decry the use of demonic slave labour.
