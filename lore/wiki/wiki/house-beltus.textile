_The Summoners of Conjuration_

A hoard of magically summoned servants perform the bulk of the city's labour - an uncompensated workforce that drives the more mundane manufacturing and service industries. The most visible of these are the shadowy horses that can be seen drawing cabs and carts throughout the city. House Beltus are responsible for summoning and controlling these entities, as well as the creation of teleportation gates for rapid transportation.

h3. The [[Beltan Institute | Beltan Institute]]

House Beltus maintains the [[Beltan Institute | Beltan Institute]] as its official centre for regulated summoning, binding, banishment, and pact-work. The Institute is located on [[The Canted Terrace | the Canted Terrace]] in the upper levels of [[Sindrel | Sindrel]], close enough to power to be respectable but carefully screened from [[Merrylane | Merrylane]] and the richest neighbourhoods.

The Institute's existence is a point of pride for House Beltus: proof that even the most dangerous forms of calling can be made disciplined, supervised, and useful.

h3. Fashion

House Beltus garments are dominated by black and gold, mostly because the black makes the gold stand out. Black silk, velvet, sheer smoke-coloured veils, gold embroidery, gilt chains, amber, jet, onyx, and lacquered ceremonial accessories. Their robes and coats tend to be long, vertical, and dramatic, with flowing outer robes, narrow fitted underlayers, high collars, sash-belts, jeweled cuffs, embroidered panels, and trailing fabric designed.

The decoration is dense and deliberate. Arabesque patterns, geometric borders, floral scrollwork, and gold-thread sigils run along hems, sleeves, collars, and veils.

Beltus presents power through excess. No self respecting Factor of the house would ever be seen in something that didn't require and entire team of servants to get into - and announcing that they have the capacity to conjure such a team is the exact point. A more senior summoner may wear a garment so elaborate that it requires the action of unseen servants or bound entities simply to enter a room without snagging on the furniture.

h3. Architecture

House Beltus architecture is built to impress, disarm, and dominate. Where [[House Meness | House Meness]] builds in white stone to proclaim order, House Beltus builds in black marble, gold tile, perfumed shadow, and impossible luxury. Its buildings are palaces before they are offices, theatres before they are courts, and sanctuaries before they are workshops.

The House favours great domes, high iwans, scalloped arches, tiled courtyards, reflecting pools, pierced screens, slender towers, and deep ceremonial gateways. These features are rendered in black stone, obsidian, dark lacquered wood, gold inlay, amber glass, lapis, onyx, and patterned tilework. Walls and floors often gleam as though freshly polished, regardless of how much traffic passes through them.

A Beltan building rarely offers a direct route from one place to another. Corridors bend through scented chambers, curtained galleries, ornamental gardens, reception courts, and pools of black water bright with gold lamps. The impracticality is deliberate. Nobody important in House Beltus is expected to carry, fetch, scrub, open, guard, sweep, or wait. Summoned beings, bound servants, invisible hands, and lesser spirits make the architecture usable. The building itself is an argument: power means never having to touch the world directly.

Public Beltan architecture favours spectacle. Audience halls are tall enough to make petitioners look small. Balconies and screened galleries allow senior Callers to observe without being approached. Incense, silk curtains, hanging lamps, and low music are as much a part of the architecture as stone and mortar.

The House symbol is displayed in black against gold, or gold against black, above portals, on banners, in tile mosaics, and in the metalwork of gates and screens. In Beltus, beauty is not decoration. It is policy. A visitor is meant to feel honoured, overawed, seduced, and trapped before a single word is spoken.

h3. Anathemist Sabotage

In recent months, House Beltus suffered a grotesque magical incident now believed to be part of the wider [[Anathemists | Anathemist]] sabotage campaign. On 14th Ostory, a summoning being carried out by [[:caller-georg | Caller Johann Georg]] went badly wrong. The demon broke free of the summoning circle and possessed the summoner. Under its influence, [[:caller-georg | Caller Georg]] ran amok through the halls of the [[Beltan Institute | Beltan Institute]], defacing portraits and other precious items before his heart finally gave out.

As a final sick joke, the demon enchanted its own sulphurous leavings, which exploded after being gathered up with the rest of the day's refuse. The page of [[:caller-georg | Caller Georg]]'s newly purchased grimoire showing the appropriate summoning circle for a succubus was later found to have been vandalised.

Information recently uncovered by [[The Adventurers | the Wonch]] suggests that this event may have been a cover for the theft of a large quantity of sulfur.

h3. Philosophy

House Beltus concerns itself with the arts of summoning and command. To House Beltus, civilisation exists because capable individuals impose order upon a chaotic world. The ability to command is not merely a skill, but a virtue. A properly ordered society is one in which every individual understands their responsibilities, every servant understands their duties, and every command is obeyed.

This worldview has earned the House a reputation for arrogance. Beltus members often regard themselves as the indispensable machinery that keeps [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] functioning, and many privately believe their contributions are insufficiently appreciated by the other Houses.

h3. Headquarters

Unlike the other Great Houses, House Beltus possesses no single headquarters.

Each senior member of the House maintains their own estate, palace, or compound. To outsiders these appear as entirely separate properties scattered throughout [[Sindrel | Sindrel]]. Internally, however, they are linked by an extensive network of teleportation gates disguised as ordinary doors.

A Beltus noble may leave their study, open a seemingly mundane door, and emerge in another estate on the opposite side of the city. Meeting chambers, libraries, audience halls, and private offices may all be reached in this manner.

As a result, the leaders of House Beltus have little need for a central headquarters. They can walk into one another's offices as easily as walking into the next room.

h3. The Doors

The teleportation doors of House Beltus are governed by the House's hierarchy.
A door may be barred against equals or subordinates, allowing the summoners a degree of privacy. However, a sufficiently senior summoner may always override such restrictions.
This arrangement allows every Beltus noble to maintain the appearance of sovereignty while ensuring that no member of the House is ever truly beyond the authority of their superiors.
