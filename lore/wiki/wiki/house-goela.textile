_The Oracles._

House Goela are masters of the school of Divination. Of all the [[Sindrel | great houses]], their role is the most ephemeral, but possibly the most important - they provide guidance, foresight, and advice to all within the city.

h3. Known Factors

h2. Fashion

House Goela clothing favours flowing, layered garments in purple, violet, dusk-blue, white, silver, and black. Wrapped mantles, belted tunics, shoulder-pinned robes, pleated skirts or panels, and long draped himations are typical. In deference to [[Sindrel | Sindrel]]’s cold these are often paired with quilted underlayers, lined cloaks, and heavy shawls embroidered with constellations, omen-marks, and branching decision-patterns.

Unlike Beltus, Goela does not dress for luxury. Unlike Mabon, it does not dress for work. Goela dresses for meaning. A mantle may record a lineage of fulfilled predictions. A border pattern may indicate a school of divination. A silver clasp may mark the wearer as a witness, interpreter, oracle, or House Factor. Even failed prophecies may be embroidered into ceremonial garments, preserved as warnings against certainty.

Formal garments often use layered drapery: pale inner robes beneath darker mantles, veils over bright linings, and shawls embroidered with stars, branching paths, eclipse marks, or fragments of old predictions. The Goela glyph is usually worn at the shoulder, brow, throat, or hem, places associated with sight, speech, and burden.

Masks and veils are common. Among Goela, a covered face does not necessarily hide identity; it marks distance from ordinary perception. Seers may wear translucent veils, silver eye-bands, ritual blindfolds, or smooth masks that echo the  [[Mask of Foreknowledge | Mask]] worn in depictions of their founder. Senior oracles sometimes appear with their eyes entirely hidden, forcing petitioners to wonder whether they are being seen more clearly or not at all.

Interpreters and Factors dress more soberly, in heavy purple civic robes, annotated sashes, silver clasps, and practical winter mantles, but a Goelan in full ceremonial dress is intended to look less like a private citizen than a public instrument: a body arranged so that the future may speak through it, and so that nobody can be quite sure who is doing the speaking.

h2. Architecture

Like [[House Oestra | House Oestra]], House Goela's  architecture descends from antiquity. In contrast to the more martial house, however, the diviners echo the styles of temples, oracle sanctuaries, philosophical schools, and civic courts rather than fortresses or palaces. Where Oestra build their architecture around a core of reinforced utilitarian structures, Goela try to create open, welcoming spaces. Internal divisions are typically created via drapes of purple silk rather than walls.

Goelan buildings favour pale marble, warm limestone, purple-veined stone, silver inlay, stepped terraces, colonnades, pediments,circular chambers, and broad consultation halls. [[Sindrel | Sindrel]]'s climate and magical construction have altered these familiar forms into something stranger and grander. Colonnades are often enclosed with glass or crystal. Oracle courts are roofed against snow while still admitting moonlight and starlight. Marble floors are warmed from below. Bronze mirrors and dark pools are built into chambers to catch reflections of stars, lamps, faces, and things that may not yet have happened. The style is clean, formal, and deliberately legible: prophecy presented as public institution.

A Goelan building often feels like a temple, courthouse, academy, and observatory occupying the same body. Citizens come to seek forecasts, interpretations, auspicious dates, dream-readings, omen rulings, and official judgements on uncertain matters. Public consultation chambers are arranged like small theatres, with petitioners below and interpreters above. Archives hold prophecies, failed predictions, omen registers, birth charts, weather signs, and records of decisions made under uncertainty.

The House's more private architecture is stranger still. Inner Goelan spaces are built to manage dangerous perception: windowless oracle vaults, circular prophecy rooms, echo chambers, star-domes, mask galleries, and corridors that seem to lengthen or shorten according to the importance of the question being asked. Some archives are arranged not by date, but by possible outcome. Some doors are built in threes. Some rooms have seats for speakers who are not present yet.

The Trisybiline has shaped much of Goela's inner architecture. Chambers of three, triple screens, matched alcoves, mirrored podiums, and veiled galleries allow prophecy, interpretation, and performance to blur together. A visitor may hear three voices, see three silhouettes, and leave uncertain which one spoke the truth.

Goela ornament favours laurel, branching paths, stars, eclipses, silver script, smooth masks, and the House glyph carved into lintels, pediments, mosaics, and purple banners.


h2. Anathemist Sabotage

On 34th Ostory, [[:f-lar-mnementh | Tyromancer Pterath]], Factor of House Goela, was poisoned. Investigators determined that a large dosage of hallucinogens had been added to his cheese.

Pterath survived the poisoning, but has been unwilling to attempt any further acts of divination since the attack. Given House Goela's reliance on prophecy, interpretation, and foresight, the incident has been read by some as an attempt to blind the city at a moment of danger. No public conclusion has yet been reached as to how the poison was introduced, who had access to the cheese, or whether Pterath was specifically targeted or merely a target of convenience.

h2. Leadership

The house is headed by [[:farseer-sybera-goela | Farseer Goela]] and her attendants. They present themselves as a trio of identical figures, their identities obscured behind hooded robes and eyeless bronze masks. Their pronouncements are are always made in concert - each voice expressing a different aspect of fate - past, present, or future. They are known as the Trisybiline, and are capable of the most profound and complete foretellings. As one of the three ages, a replacement will be sought allowing the three to continue uninterrupted by death.

While serving, a member of the Trisybiline wears a uniform of hooded purple robe and eyeless mask - the concealment of their identitites is intended to protect them from retribution for unwelcome news.

In actuality the Trisybiline serves two purposes - to disguise the fact that Syberia Goela has been a member of the trio for over 1000 years, and to allow the other two members to cover for the fact that she can no longer distinguish her visions from her surroundings. In return for this service, they are able to iggyback on her prodigious power. The current sybillas are Phyto and Heraclides.

h2. Headquarters

The Pythiol appears from without to be a large ring of columns with purple silk draped between. Once a person enters the structure they find it to be a winding labyrinth of twisting routes, an effect heightened by a profusion of braziers burning psychoactive substances. Making it into the chamber of the Trisybiline is the first tests that a person must pass to be worthy of a reading by the oracles.

The Pythiol is on one of the upper reaches of the city. The closest osteomantic archive is a short bridge away. It is a common practice for those becoming factors of the house to reserve their alcove in advance.

bq). The bridge to the Pythiol arcs over a dizzying drop through several layers of the city. Most visitors cross it rather carefully, partly because of the height and partly because House Goela's clients tend to spend the journey contemplating possible futures, many of which involved falling off bridges.
<br>
Ahead stands the headquarters of the House itself. The Pythiol appears to be nothing more complex than a circle of white columns draped with curtains of purple silk that shift and billow in the high winds. Sweetly scented smoke curls around the structure, obscuring almost as much as the curtains. It looks less like a building than a stage waiting for a performance.
<br>
A shorter bridge branches away from the main platform to an smaller structure. Even constructed with Goela's characteristic pale limestone and fluted columns, you recognise the form of an Osteomantic Archive. There is a persistent rumour that aspiring factors of House Goela are expected to reserve their alcove in advance.
<br>
A queue has formed before the curtains. Merchants seeking favourable ventures, nobles seeking advantageous marriages, and gamblers seeking reassurance all wait patiently together. [[The Gods | The gods]], fate, destiny and random chance have spent millennia proving that they dislike being questioned. Yet people persisted, largely because the alternative was not knowing. One by one they file between two of the columns where the curtains have been drawn back. Within a few steps they have vanished into the ever-shifting confusion of coiling smoke and billowing curtains within.

Interior:

bq). The moment you step through the purple curtains, the outside world vanishes. The purple drapes seems to swallow the sounds of the city, leaving only the sussurus of silk sliding on silk, surrounding you with a thousand whispers on every side. If you listen closely you can almost convince yourselves that there aren't words among them.
<br>
The air is thick with smoke from hundreds of braziers set in alcoves and niches throughout the structure. The smoke smells different with every breath. One moment it carries hints of cinnamon, rain-soaked stone and old books. The next it suggests citrus, funeral flowers and a childhood memory that may never have happened. You find yourself stuck by a strange feeling of nostalgia for events you have not yet experienced.
<br>
The light offers no assistance whatsoever. The braziers cast pools of violet, gold and blue illumination that overlapped in strange ways. Shadows stretch where there should be none and vanish where they ought to have gathered. Figures glimpsed at the far end of a corridor might be pilgrims, servants, prophets or one's own reflection arriving slightly ahead of schedule.


The chamber of the Trisybiline.

bq). The chamber of the Trisybiline is said to occupy the exact centre of the Pythiol, a fact of considerable significance to the House and absolutely no help whatsoever to anyone trying to find it.
<br>
After what feels like several days, three existential crises and at least one encounter with a corridor that appears to be moving in the opposite direction, you emerge into a large circular hall. After the claustrophobic, twisting routes of the Pythiol, it feels strangely exposed. The ever-present purple drapes rise in billowing curves to the limit of your sight. How tall was the structure from the outside? You can't remember. You suspect it doesn't matter. Above you is a velvet black sky studded with shining silver stars. A ceiling? An illusion? Both, or neither.
<br>
The floor is a great mosaic of silver and purple stone depicting concentric circles, spirals and symbols of great power and importance.
<br>
At the centre stand three thrones of pale crystal, and upon them, illuminated only by the light of possibly illusory stars, sit the Trisybiline.
<br>
They wear heavy robes of deep purple that conceal every feature of their bodies. Their faces are hidden behind smooth silver masks devoid of eyes, mouths or any other recognisable human characteristic.
<br>
The silence within the chamber is thick, textured. It settles over you like a heavy blanket woven from expectation. The smoke from the labyrinth still linger faintly in the air. Then one of the masks would tilts ever so slightly. The movement is tiny. Barely noticeable but for the almost oppressive stillness. And a voice speaks. Not loudly, barely a whisper but clearly audible in the silence. Perhaps the one who moved is the one who speaks. Perhaps not.
<br>
It says "You are expected."
