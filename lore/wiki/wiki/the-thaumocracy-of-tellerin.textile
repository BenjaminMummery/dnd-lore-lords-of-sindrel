The Thaumocratic Empire of Tellerin once covered much of the south of the continent. They are typically remembered as being a dark and threatening force, famous for their use of necromancers in battle.

Following their failed attempt to invade [[The Magedom of Houd | The Magedom of Houd]] in [[World History | 3958]] [[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]], the empire withdrew. Most of the outer colonies were abandoned and the imperial core closed its borders to outsiders. Of their current political or social structure little is known.

h2. History

**3958 SC** (~260 years ago)

The death of magic was felt more swiftly and keenly in the south, which prompted the Thaumocracy to push north. Emperor Virkine III launched an invasion of [[The Magedom of Houd | The Magedom of Houd]] — the [[World History | Seven Day War]].

The decisive battle was fought on the Planes of Herrman, where a stalemate between the attacking Tellerites and the beleaguered Houdians was ended by the timely arrival of Houdian reinforcements. The bulk of the Tellerite forces were delayed by the Battle of Brassmeade Bridge, where [[:nakoma-deathwalker-mor-got-ha | Nakoma Deathwalker Mor'got'ha]] and a handful of goliath warriors held the narrow crossing for **ten days** before the bridge was destroyed; she was slain and later resurrected by [[:nitherfang-gilfrarrin | Nitherfang Gilfrarrin]].

Following defeat, Virkine was assassinated by his grand vizier Hurletoros the Black, sparking a civil war that has ravaged what remains of the empire. Imperial forces still man the borders, but the Thaumocracy is deeply insular.

h2. Political Structure

The position of Emperor of Tellerin was a lifetime appointment chosen by a contest of magical power, making the empire theoretically a meritocracy.
