h3. Religion in Sindrel

Sindrel has long treated religion as tolerated eccentricity: useful for funerals, private comfort, and old family rites, but faintly embarrassing in serious civic life. Since the Anathemist threat became public, explicitly religious practice has become politically suspect, because many Sindrelians conflate devotion, anti-magical sentiment, and Anathemist fanaticism.

h3. The Houdian Pantheon

Northern [[Artresh | Artresh]] was, until approximately 250 years ago, united under the [[The Magedom of Houd | The Magedom of Houd]]. During their expansion, the Houdians found it expedient to syncretise local religions into their own. Any local creator deity, for example, would be regarded as an aspect of the Houdian Dawn Father, and most if not all local religious customs would continue unchallenged. This allowed them to present their own religious teachings as an extension of existing beliefs, rather than a replacement.

Following the dissolution of the Magedom, this practice has resulted in a religious landscape at once unified and highly variable. While the identities of the gods are largely universally consistent, which are specifically venerated and how can vary wildly even between neighbouring regions. Some individual practices date back to long before the Magedom. Two priests may both venerate the Dawn Father, but one might be making offerings of grain and fruit to his aspect as the Bringer of the Harvest, while another sacrifices a goat to the Arbiter of Justice.

h4. The Dawn Father

A creator god, and typically considered head honcho. Various traditions regard him as either a singular original creator and leader of the other gods by dint of having given rise to them, or as a 'first among equals' who proved himself through might and skill. Associated with leadership, authority, justice, the harvest, the sun, and creation itself.

h4. The Fate Weaver

The deity of art, craftspersonship, blacksmiths, and foresight.

h4. The Queen of Ravens

The goddess of death, vengeance, destruction, night, and fear.

Those who worship the Carrion Lady do so secretly and cautiously - her true name is spoken only in direst need or by her most devout follows as it is believed that attracting her direct attention is tantamount to suicide.

h4. The Sage of Heaven

God of knowledge, teaching, writing, and memory.

h4. The Redeemer 

God of innocence, purity, martyrdom, self-sacrifice, morning twilight, and midwifery.

h4. The Grey Wanderer

God of journeys, pilgrimage, exploration, and self-discovery.

h4. The Storm Warden

Deity of protection, caution, duty, and righteous war.

h4. The Green Lady

Goddess of nature, fertility, the earth, and decay

h4. The Laughing Maw

Deity of trickery, deceit, rebellion, chaos, and invention.
