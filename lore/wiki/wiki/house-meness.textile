House Meness are primarily tasked with maintaining both the magical defences around [[Sindrel | Sindrel]], and the rule of law within them. They are its guardsmen, jailers, and defence force.

House Meness are often paired with [[House Oestra | House Oestra]] as both have a strong martial bent.

h3. Architecture

House Meness buildings are designed to look lawful before they look beautiful. They are pale, symmetrical, and highly visible: white stone walls, dark timber framing, black iron hardware, and pale banners bearing the Meness symbol. Their civic buildings often borrow the long rooflines and heavy timberwork of old northern halls, but refine them into something more official: watch-houses, courts, gate stations, tribunal halls, and precinct fortresses.

A Meness building usually has a strong central axis. The main doors are broad and dark, often iron-bound, set beneath a shield-shaped lintel or carved House glyph. Roofs are steep and practical, designed to shed rain and snow. Towers are square, watchful, and blunt rather than romantic. Windows are narrow but orderly, arranged in disciplined rows. The whole structure seems to say: you may enter, but you will be seen.

Decoration is restrained. Knotwork appears along cornices, thresholds, shield-bosses, and column capitals. Runic inscriptions are carved into stone bands above doors, recording laws, oaths, judgements, or memorials to officers killed in service. Shield motifs appear everywhere: above gates, on banners, on wall plaques, and in the shape of lanterns.

Inside, Meness architecture is bright, echoing, and severe. Public halls have pale walls, dark rafters, polished floors, and benches arranged with intimidating clarity. Watch rooms, armouries, holding cells, evidence vaults, and hearing chambers are built into the same ordered plan. Abjurative wards are not hidden; they are carved into thresholds and floor borders like civic ornament.

The best Meness buildings feel protective and oppressive at the same time. They are shelters, courts, barracks, and warnings.

h3. Uniforms of The Watch

House Meness uniforms are severe, bright, and instantly recognisable. Their base uniform is a long white or off-white high-collared tunic-coat, cut close through the torso and falling to mid-thigh or knee depending on rank. The coat fastens with a vertical line of pale metal buttons, giving the wearer a formal, constabulary silhouette. The shoulders are squared but not flamboyant, often reinforced with hidden abjurative plates beneath the cloth. The tunic is accessorised with black details - A broad leather belt from which hands a shortsword and truncheon; gloves of black calfskin; charcoal trousers tucked into high polished black leather boots; the trident of House Meness embroidered into the back.

The white uniform is not practical in the ordinary sense; that is the point. A Meness officer is expected to appear clean, controlled, and visible. Dirt on the uniform is tolerated only when earned in service.

The House's ancient lineage shows through in disciplined details. Coats are edged with subtle woven knotwork. Rank is marked by small runic bars at the collar, and shoulder-tabs. Senior officers may wear fur-lined weather-cloaks clasped with shield-brooches, especially outdoors or in ceremonial settings. Their helmets, when worn, are rounded, inspired by old nasal helms but simplified into civic headgear. On patrol, many instead wear a stiff white constable’s cap with a dark band and a Meness shield badge at the front.

House Meness armour is integrated rather than ostentatious. A patrol uniform might include a polished breastplate under the coat, bracers hidden beneath sleeves, or a reinforced gorget at the throat. Riot or crisis gear is more obvious: white-lacquered shields, pale helmets, layered lamellar panels, and long batons or short spears. Even then, the impression is not of warriors charging into battle, but of a line that will not break.

Worn over the heart is a silver badge shaped like a shield - the official mark of an officer of Sindrel's law.

h3. Headquarters

The Panopticon serves as the headquarters of [[House Meness | House Meness]] — a floating fortress that circles [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] continuously.

bq). Unlike the fixed estates of the other Houses, the Panopticon hangs in the air above [[Sindrel | Sindrel]], drifting on a slow, endless circuit of the city. From the outside, the headquarters of House Meness resemble a colosseum - a circular building almost as tall as it is wide. The outer surface is windowless. A wide gate leads to a tunnel-like passageway that takes visitors through the thickness of the outer structure and into the central plaza.
<br>
From within, you can see that the outer structure is much narrower at the top - the inner walls slope away from the plaza in a gentle arc, as though the entire colossal structure was a cup into which should rest a giant ball. The tiered balconies sparkle with windows and balconies, although these grow increasingly reinforced and fortified towards the top of the structure.
<br>
The core of the building is The Watchtower half as high again as the outer walls.
<br>
Within its walls are barracks, prisons, courtrooms, administrative offices, and command facilities. Its unhurried patrol overhead serves as a visible reminder of House Meness's responsibility for law, security, and order.

h3. Anathemist Sabotage

On _17th Enean_, [[:major-vindas | Major Vindas]] was killed by a piece of armour with an inverted reflection enchantment. Instead of reversing incoming force, the enchantment amplified it. When she invited an aide to help test the armour with a low-level firebolt spell, she was incinerated instantly.

The death of Major Vindas has deepened fears that the [[Anathemists | Anathemists]] are not merely attacking buildings or institutions, but turning ordinary magical safeguards into lethal traps.

h3. Ranks

House Meness operates a strict hierarchy, with a clear path to House membership. Adjutants start as recruits and gradually ascend to the rank of Serjeant Major. Their next promotion, to the rank of Ensign, brings them into the House proper. From there one can theoretically ascend all the way to the rank of General and official leadership of the House itself.
