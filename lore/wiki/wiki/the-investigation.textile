h2. GM meta

The public wiki states the Hexine Circle assumes Anathemist spies are responsible. GM truth: evidence does not yet rule out another faction, a false-flag operation, or [[Anathemists | Anathemists]] using non-obvious proxies.

h2. The Spies

* [[:barnabus | Barnabus]] - butler; **cell lead / coordinator**
* [[:georges-de-selve | Georges de Selve]] - merchant; senior lieutenant (logistics, quartermaster, strike team)
* [[:jean-de-dinteville | Jean de Dinteville]] - merchant; senior lieutenant (social intel, target selection)
* [[:arouraios | Arouraios]] - servant / cartographer; reports to Barnabus
* [[:regnif-d-log | Regnif d'Log]] - patsy

Barnabus runs the embedded half of the cell (retainer cover, Sacrum staging, stay-behind). Georges and Jean execute merchant-cover operations and the Ditchquer climax on his timeline; Jean's timings and Georges's supply runs implement Barnabus's plan rather than a separate agenda.

h2. The Heist

h3. Setup

* Enter the city as small groups - Regnif d'Log] and his servants [[:arouraios | Arouraios]] and [[:barnabus | Barnabus]] as one group, the merchants [[:georges-de-selve | Georges de Selve]] and [[:jean-de-dinteville | Jean de Dinteville]] as another.
* Use the criminals around the city to infiltrate the garbage collection. This gives them a way to move materials around the city.
* [[:barnabus | Barnabus]], supposedly acting as an agent of [[:regnif-d-log | d'Log]], made a deal with a magical armourer ([[kelferria-the-smith | Kelferria]] of [[House Mabon | House Mabon]]) for a large order of enchanted armour. He made a large down payment with promises of a lot more.  Kelferria produced a few samples, which is what he actually wanted. This serves two purposes: protection within the [[Quarantine Zone | quarantine zone]] so they can use The [[Quarantine Zone | Sacrum]] as their staging ground, and disguises - the armorer supplies [[House Oestra | House Oestra]], so the chest-plates, once clumsily decorated with the oestra crest, will pass as Oestra at a distance.

h3. Gathering Supplies

* The sabotage on [[House Beltus | House Beltus]]  provides cover to steal sulfur - Beltus produce a bunch as a by-product of summoning so there's plenty going. They wait nearby in the cart, head over when they hear the chaos, empty the sulfur silo into the cart then set a fire in the silo. Sulfer burns well so it covers the theft, and in the chaos it gets blamed on the demon mishap.
* The sabotage on [[House Grimbolg | House Grimbolg]] provides cover to steal Nitre (it dissolves in water)
* Charcoal can be bought as a fuel around the city (although [[:regnif-d-log | d'Log]] has noticed that his lodgings appear to be particularly cold).
* They identify and kidnap [[:nielsenn | Nielsenn]], staging the explosion in his shop to suggest his death.
* The remaining acts of sabotage are to provide cover for the thefts and to create a sense of paranoia.
* The charcoal, nitre, and sulfur are accumulated at the [[Quarantine Zone | Sacrum]].

h3. The [[Ditchquer | Ditchquer]] Closing Ceremony

* [[:georges-de-selve | Georges de Selve]] and [[:jean-de-dinteville | Jean de Dinteville]] plant the explosives.
* At the [[Ditchquer | Ditchquer]] Closing ceremony, [[:barnabus | Barnabus]] lifts [[:silas-oestra | Silas Oestra]]'s key and passes it off to [[:arouraios | Arouraios]] who is disguised as a coachman.
* [[:georges-de-selve | Georges de Selve]] and [[:jean-de-dinteville | Jean de Dinteville]]  emerge from the tunnels and colect the key from  [[:arouraios | Arouraios]]
* They collect the [[:nielsenn| Nielsenn]] and make their way into the catacombs.
* The first explosive detonates. Reinforcements are summoned from [[House Oestra | House Oestra]], leaving them understaffed.
* [[:georges-de-selve | Georges de Selve]], [[:jean-de-dinteville | Jean de Dinteville]] and the locksmith [[:nielsenn| Nielsenn]] emerge from the archives within the [[House Oestra | leaguer]]. In the dark, the symbol painted on their armour passes as House Oestra.
* They make their way to the keep, using the key and locksmith to access [[:silas-oestra | Silas's]] rooms. They copy what they can and steal what they can't, then leave.
* [[:georges-de-selve | Georges de Selve]] and [[:jean-de-dinteville | Jean de Dinteville]] take [[:nielsenn| Nielsenn]] back to the hq, where they kill him. They then leave the city, returning to the disguise as merchants.
* 35 days later, they return for more 'merchanting'. Barnabus, as part of d'Log's retinue, has been permitted to remain until his master's business is concluded.

h3. Kosmios and elite panic

The anathemists did not know the word **Kosmios** at the time of the Fulcrum bombing. The cell learned it only after [[:barnabus | Barnabus]] read material stolen from [[:silas-oestra | Silas]]'s rooms during the heist.

When the higher-ups learned from Hielaman that the Anathemist Lieutenant had dropped the word 'Kosmios', House elite **panicked**: if the enemy knew **Kosmios**, the Fulcrum attack might have been an attempt on the **rift** beneath the city, not opportunistic terror. That reframing is **partly wrong** — the Fulcrum was chosen as **diversion**; the bombers did not yet understand what they were adjacent to. It is **partly right** — the cell now wants [[Dockward | Dockward]] as the real lever.

The [[Hexine Circle | Hexine Circle]] has **not** been told this interpretation. They still treat the bombing as sabotage against civic infrastructure.
