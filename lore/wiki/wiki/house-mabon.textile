As the only place where it is possible to create them, [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] has an effective monopoly on enchanted items. These form the core of its economic power, and House Mabon are the people who create them.

h2. Fashion and Workwear

House Mabon clothing descends from forms worn in the eastern lands, but centuries in cold [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] have made it thicker, layered, and more practical. Where Beltus uses clothing to display effortless command, and Oestra uses armour to declare martial duty, Mabon dresses for making.

The standard Mabon silhouette is layered and asymmetrical: wrapped coats, short work-kimono, insulated haori-like jackets, split aprons, broad sashes, reinforced collars, and loose trousers tucked into boots. Fabrics are warm and durable: heavy indigo wool, quilted linen, lacquered leather, padded silk, felt, fur edging, and dense woven cloth treated against sparks, cold, acid, or stray enchantment.

The arms and hands are kept as free as possible. Sleeves are shortened, tied back, slit, or buttoned out of the way. Cuffs are reinforced but close-fitting. Gloves are used only when needed, and even then are thin, articulated, or fingerless. A Mabon artisan would rather endure cold than lose precision at the fingertips.

Ornamentation is present, but it is never idle. Decorative stitching marks rank, lineage, workshop, or completed masterworks. A beautiful clasp is also a tool-lock. A lacquered bead may be a spell focus. A patterned sash may contain hidden measuring marks. The House glyph appears as a maker's mark: embroidered at the shoulder, stamped into a tool belt, worked into a clasp, or stitched inside a garment where another craftsperson would know to look.

Mabon colours favour deep blue, steel blue, storm grey, frost white, silver, black, and muted brass. Fine dress is more elaborate, but still restrained. A Master Craftsman may wear flowing ceremonial layers and exquisite embroidery, but the cut still leaves the hands free. Even at a formal function, a Mabon looks as though they could repair the chandelier, rebind the door wards, and improve the seating arrangements before the second course.

For House Mabon, elegance is not excess. Elegance is function so refined that it becomes beautiful.

h2. Architecture

The echoes of the eastern lands remain in House Mabon's architecture, but the cold climate and magical construction have made it heavier, warmer, and more technically ambitious. It is architecture built by craftspeople for craftspeople: layered, adaptable, precise, and quietly beautiful.

Mabon buildings favour blue-glazed roof tiles, dark treated timber, pale stone foundations, carved brackets, covered walkways, and deep eaves designed to shed snow and rain. Their structures preserve the grace of the old forms, but with thicker walls, insulated cores, enclosed courtyards, heated floors, storm shutters, and magically regulated chimneys to draw smoke, fumes, and stray enchantment safely away from the work.

The House's buildings are rarely static. Internal rooms are created with lightweight paper-and-wood screens reinforced with enchantment, easily moved as the needs of the occupants change. A room used for drafting in the morning may become a binding chamber by evening, then a demonstration hall the following day. This flexibility is not disorder; it is craft expressed as architecture.

Mabon ornamentation is restrained but exacting. Joinery, bracket-work, metal inlay, lacquered beams, carved tool motifs, and geometric patterns reveal the maker's hand without overwhelming the function of the space. The House glyph appears on blue banners, workshop signs, bridge plaques, roof tiles, tool cabinets, and discreet seals above doors. In Mabon, decoration is not added after usefulness. It is usefulness refined until it becomes beautiful.

[[Dockward | Dockward]] shows this style at its largest scale. Terraced workshops, forge halls, enchantment chambers, design galleries, apprentice wards, market halls, and storehouses interlock in a dense, purposeful network. Smoke from kilns and forges rises through enchanted vents. Blue banners hang above narrow bridges. Warm light spills from workshops late into the night.

A Mabon district does not try to intimidate or dazzle. It hums. It measures. It improves. Every beam is set, every joint is considered, and every space exists so that something can be imagined, shaped, repaired, taught, or made.

h2. Headquarters

The Prow, on the seaward rim of [[Dockward | Dockward]], serves as the headquarters of House Mabon.

bq). Atop [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] hangs [[Dockward | Dockward]], a mile-wide ring of arches, cranes, and bays that serves as the city's principal airship port. Colloquially it is called the annulus, for the halo it forms around the upper reaches of [[The Fulcrum | the Fulcrum]]. On the seaward side of the ring stands the building known as The Prow. Its name is well earned: the walls sweep steeply outwards, creating a structure resembling nothing more than the bow of a colossal ship jutting out over the waves far below. From this high seat, House Mabon oversees a trade empire that covers much of the continent.

h2. Anathemist Sabotage

House Mabon has suffered at least two suspicious incidents linked to the wider Anathemist sabotage campaign.

The first occurred on _8th Ostory_, when [[:nielsenn | Adjutant Nielsenn]] was vaporised after one of his own trapped locks misfired. Nielsenn was a skilled craftsman and locksmith, making the nature of the accident especially troubling. Whether this was a tragic failure, sabotage, or something more deliberate remains a subject of concern.

The second occurred on _8th Enean_. [[:craftsman-shegrim | Craftsman Shegrim]] was anonymously commissioned to place a spell of reflection on an amulet. The amulet had already been enchanted with a spell of reflection, causing the enchantment to rebound. In a city where magic is everywhere, Shegrim has become a walking danger to everyone around him.

Together, these incidents suggest that House Mabon's expertise in craft, enchantment, locks, armour, and magical objects has made it a particular target. The House's own tools and specialities have been turned against it.
