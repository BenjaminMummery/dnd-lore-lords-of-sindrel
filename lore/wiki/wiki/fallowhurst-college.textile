A ruined college located in [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]].

The Fallowhusrt College of Medicinal Studies, more commonly simply styled as "[[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]] College" was an institution in the village of [[Fallowhurst | Fallowhurst]], half a day's travel south-west of [[Sindrel | Sindrel]]. The buildings the college occupied were originally constructed as a grand estate for a noble family before being remodelled and repurposed.

h3. Mysteries and Clues

* At some point in the past, the village was abandoned and the college fell into disrepair. Whether these two events were linked remains to be seen.
* At some stage the college were using [[The Fallowhurst Sigil | The Fallowhurst Sigil]]. The intended purpose remains unclear.
* The college appears to be connected to [[:embren-oestra | The Plague Doctor]].

Originally the estate of [[House Oestra | House Oestra]], it was converted to a medical college at the direction of [[:embren-oestra | Embren Oestra]].

h3. Key Rooms

h4. Lecture Hall (C)

bq). This room runs the full three-storey height of the building, and is ringed by a pair of partially collapsed galleries accessed from the upper floors. Tall windows on the west wall once allowed the sun to illuminate the space, but their vacant frames have been almost overtaken by the ivy that once grew on the outside wall and is now colonising the interior. In what little light is admitted illuminates the remains of wooden pew-like benches that litter the floor. At the center is a portable lectern and blackboard, whatever writing it once bore long-since washed away by the water dripping from a large crack in the vaulted ceiling.

h4. Operating Theatre (D)

bq). Three stories above you, the remains of a glass dome, now little more than a lattice of corroded ironwork, lets in a column of daylight that illuminates only the center of the room, leaving the rest shrouded in shadow. At its focus is an adjustable wooden bench adorned with rotten leather straps. Next to it stands a wheeled table, the light glinting from the tarnished knives, saws, and needles arrayed neatly on its top.
<br>
As your eyes adjust, you can make out the tiered galleries that ring the walls. Each and every seat is occupied - wherever you turn your gaze is met with the empty eye-sockets and ivory grins of hundreds of skeletons.

h4. Laboratory (K)

bq). Something has been growing in here. The window isn't just obscured by plant life, but choked by it. Vines and creepers are draped between every surface like cobwebs. Between them you make out the shapes pestles, alembics, and flasks, all of them cracked or shattered.

h3. Return Visits

The party's first visit ([[Session 2: Fallowhurst | Session 2]]) covered the village, orchard, entrance hall, preparation room, lecture hall, and museum. They triggered the [[The Fallowhurst Sigil | binding sigil]] in the lecture hall and on Carlos Winterborn's body; chanting from the Operating Theatre (D) ended the session. Most of the college - the theatre itself, Embren's suite, and the upper floors - remains unexplored.

Likely return motives: seek out a source of necromantic power for the [[The Octarion Circle | Octarion Circle]]; lay [[:embren-oestra | the Plague Doctor]] to rest; undo the [[The Fallowhurst Sigil | Fallowhurst sigil]].

h4. What's Changed Since Session 2

* The grime trail still runs A → B → D → E. It looks fresher if Embren has passed through recently.
* Scholar-skeletons in the Operating Theatre may be awake or agitated after the sigil discharge.
* Museum displays may have been rearranged if Embren visited between sessions.
* Assume upper floors and most side rooms are untouched unless you decide otherwise.

h4. [[:embren-oestra | Embren Oestra]] (X-factor)

Do not assume Embren is on-site when the party arrives. Decide at the start of each visit - or when they re-enter the estate - whether he is here at all. He may be lost in the [[Osteomantic Archives | catacombs]], hunting the party elsewhere, or keeping to the village fringe. An absent Embren turns the college into a slow-burn dungeon (scholars, sigil echoes, fresh trails) rather than a scripted chase.

If he *is* present, pick a location and activity; he does not need to seek the party out immediately.

| Roll | Location | Activity |
| 1 | *Absent* | Catacombs or off-site - see [[Osteomantic Archives | Osteomantic Archives]] GM |
| 2 | Village | Making rounds among empty houses; ignores hails |
| 3 | Orchard / greenhouses | Tending [[Triffid | triffids]]; avoids [[:tama | Tama]] |
| 4 | Operating Theatre (D) | Chanting at the bench; "lecturing" the skeleton audience |
| 5 | Museum (E) | Adjusting labels or preparing a new display |
| 6 | Embren's Suite (X) or Laboratory (K) | Reading [[:letter-from-lord-oestra-to-embren-oestra | Lord Oestra's letter]]; feeding the [[Triffid | triffid]] |

When the party do encounter him: shallow phrases from his former life ("We'll get that fixed right up." "The best care on the continent."). Given opportunity, he attempts to strap a victim down in the Operating Theatre and resume the binding procedure. He may stalk the rear of the marching order rather than attack openly; if denied a subject for long enough, he grows desperate and attempts abduction. When killed, bound scholar-skeletons rebuild themselves over several minutes.

h4. The Wandering Scholars

The skeleton audience in the Operating Theatre are bound spirits of the college's scholars. They slumber until disturbed - movement, noise, or damage to anything they value (museum displays, lecture hall fixtures, binding marks). Observant characters may notice skulls turning to follow them or hear bone grinding before a full awakening. They are not uniformly hostile unless provoked; destroying exhibits or interrupting a binding turns them against intruders.

h4. Escalation

* *Quiet exploration:* scholars mostly slumber; the trail looks undisturbed.
* *Sigil use or museum damage:* chanting from D, skeletons stir, trail freshens - good moment to roll Embren's presence.
* *Operating Theatre entered:* full awakening likely unless the party has been exceptionally stealthy.

h4. Complications

* Healing potions may still be found in rooms the party has not searched.
* [[:tama | Tama]] may aid or warn the party in the orchard if they are struggling or making too much noise.
* Names on museum labels (e.g. Sir Cassia Drake) match plaques on private rooms - reward thorough cross-referencing.

h4. The Orchard

Originally ornamental, later repurposed for medicinal herb beds. Dangers include [[Strangle Vines | strangle vines]], [[Razorgrass | razorgrass]] (half speed or 1d4 slashing per 5 ft), and [[Sporeshrooms | sporeshrooms]]. Greenhouses hold [[Triffid | triffid]] saplings; one mature [[Triffid | triffid]] can attack. [[:tama | Tama]] lives here.

h4. Museum (E)

Rotten bodies arrayed as teaching failures. Labels grow shakier over time. Notable: _Subject 1: Carlos Winterborn_ - cervical fracture, "Partial Success" (the binding that created Embren). Early victims had life-threatening conditions; later victims were whoever was available.

The spirits bound to the skeletons are typically slumbering, but will begin to awake as [[The Adventurers | the adventurers]] move around. Observant characters may catch a skull seeming to shift towards them, or hear the grinding of bones moving. The scholars are not hostile unless the players start destroying anything they hold dear.

Grime has collected at the edges of the room, but the main floor is free of it having been repeatedly traversed by wondering scholars. The trail from the front door ends here, with a second trail leading onwards towards the Museum (E).[end-content] [end-accordion-item]
[accordion-item] [title] E. Museum  [end-title] [content]  bq). Glass display cabinets once lined the walls of this room, the shattered shards of which crunch underfoot as you enter. Whatever was once displayed here has been removed - your eyes find a tall heap of tarnished silver wear, a mulchy strata that may once have been paper, and the splintered forms of various delicate and once expensive objects in the south-west corner. In their place, the remains of the cabinets play host to a collection of rotten bodies. Corpses of various sizes and condition are arrayed for your perusal. Close to the door they are comparatively fresh, one even retains sallow flesh stretched taught over its bones. Further in they are increasingly decayed - exposed bones clad in a new life of fungus and infestation.
At the far end of the room, beneath three shattered windows, is displayed a final body. Surrounded by rot and decay it is pristine. A young man, no more than 25.

Affixed to the cabinets by each body is a piece of wood serving as a label. The older bodies are labelled in a clear, clean hand that grows increasingly shaky and unclear as the bodies get newer. Example labels are:
 * On the pristine body: "Subject 1: Carlos Winterborn. 25 M. Occupation: Noble. Affliction: Cervical Fracture. Outcome: Partial Success."
* "Subject 13: Annabell Lee. 65 F. Occupation: Weaver. Affliction: Gangerene of the leg. Outcome: Failure."
* "Subject 87: Brálunda Volk. 19 F. Occupation: Barwonch. Affliction: Glasslung. Outcome: Failure."
* The latest before the writing devolves into illegible scrawl: "Subject 415: John Doe. Approx 34 N. Occupation: Unknown (traveller). Affliction: Fractured Clavicle. Outcome: Failure."

Where it is possible to see, the bodies have injuries or other signs consistent with the described malady, but no obvious cause of death. The pristine body shows no sign of any injury or illness. Otherwise the older bodies have more serious conditions, trending towards more minor conditions for the later cases.

The wondering scholars have been using the museum as a record of their attempts. Their early victims were patients and villagers with life-threatening conditions. As the village died out and the sanity of the scholars diminished, they started taking whoever was available. All have been failures except for the first, which, depending on your perspective, was the greatest failure of all.

Several of the names can be found on private rooms or in the wards. [end-content] [end-accordion-item]

[accordion-item] [title]  K. Laboratory [end-title] [content] bq). Something has been growing in here. The window isn't just obscured by plant life, but choked by it. Vines and creepers are draped between every surface like cobwebs. Between them you make out the shapes pestles, alembics, and flasks, all of them cracked or shattered.

The vines are actually a [[Triffid | triffid]], grown from a seed that was originally used in an experiment. Characters tracking the vines to their source, or simply thoroughly searching the room, will find the body of the plant in the shattered remains of the petri dish where it was grown. [accordion-item] [title]  L. Specimen Room [end-title] [content] Storage for interesting specimens awaiting investigation in the laboratory. Samples taken from patients, interesting plants, things of this nature. The shelves are adorned with silver-inlaid runes that originally kept the samples fresh. Is their magic has waned, the samples have fallen to rot.

Several of the samples are from [[Triffid | Triffids]].

Characters entering the room must make a DC10 constitution saving throw or become sickened due to the miasma. A successful search of the room will turn up a bottle of potent poison. [end-content] [end-accordion-item]
