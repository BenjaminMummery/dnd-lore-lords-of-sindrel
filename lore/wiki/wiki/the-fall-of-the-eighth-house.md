Legend tells that the eighth founder of sindrel, the Lord of Necromancy, turned against the others and sought to destroy them. In a dark ritual, every member of the Eighth house was sacrificed, their power turning the dark lord into a world-ending threat. It took the combined might of the seven other founders to defeat this monster, but evil of this scope could not be fully destroyed. At great personal cost, they sealed the Necromancer away, hopefully forever.

Necromancy has always been a taboo art in Arcan - not entirely verboten, but its practitioners were always viewed with suspicion. The inclusion of Samhain in the research group was a grudging concession to practicality more than anything else.

This led to an ongoing separation between the necromancer and the rest of her team, a problem worsened by her disinterest in immortality and the eventual discovery that she had been concealing her vamirism. By this time, however, the Osteomantic Archives had become an established part of Sindrel, and the rest of the Octarion Circle had no choice but to be tolerant.

Matters came to a head over the plan to use the Rift Stabilisation Project, previously a way of controlling to which world the rift linked, as a mechanism to lock the rift to Kosmios. The rest of the Circle, hungry for power and resources, sought to establish a beachhead and harvest Kosmios's resources through the permanently open gateway. Samhain's opposition was vocal, and the other Lords feared she would move against them. They plotted to exploit her frequent sojourns in Thanismot, sealing the rift behind her and leaving her to ultimately starve.

Having anticipated their betrayal, however, Samhain laid her own plans. Those among her house who she could trust to be loyal had been gradually moved to Thanismot while the rest had, under the guize of an initiation to the House, been placed under a simple but powerful spell. Samhain had bound their oaths of fealty to a deadwoman switch - the instant the portal closed behind her, every necromancer remaining in Sindrel died.

Having been pretty much the only place in Arcan where necromancers could live and practice openly, Sindrel had naturally attracted a great number of them. On top of this, House Samhain had recruited aggressively. As a result, the schism represented an almost total removal of necromancy from Arcan. While scattered remnants would countinue to exist, mostly among the ruins of the Tellarin Empire, the death of magic spelled their doom - shorn of their power they were at the mercy of a world that hated and feared them.

While the world at large saw Samhain's act as one of destructive rage, she had a deeper purpose. Denied access to the Ostemoantic Archives and the knowledge and power of the necromancers, the Octarion Circle would be unable to leverage one eighth of magic, severely curtailing their power.

In the intervening centuries, the Lords of Sindrel have painted Samhain as their boogyman. In their telling, she is the great betrayer - a trusted friend who turned against them, slaughtered her house in a dark ritual, and was sealed away after a heroic battle.

As the Circel have developed their ambitions, it has become clear that with their number incomplete they will be unable to carry out the high-level reality warping required to move the entire city into Kosmios. They have begun searching for a high-level necromancer but so far have come up empty. [[:high-summoner-nial-beltus | Nial Beltus]] believes that mounting an expedition into Thanismot to recruit one of the House Samhain necromancers (if any have survived) or recover their training materials is the correct choice. He is stridently opposed by [[:general-alicia-meness | Alicia Meness]] who views Samhain as too dangerous to ever risk releasing.
