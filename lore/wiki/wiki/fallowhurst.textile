Fallowhurst, also known as "The Drowned Crossing" is an abandoned village and estate on [[The Mage Road | The Mage Road]]. The estate is the location of [[Fallowhurst College | Fallowhurst College]].

h2. Geography

The village sits within a boggy marshland formed by the flood plane of the River Deren.

h2. Construction

The village was constructed primarily of one or two story wooden buildings with stone foundations. The raised foundations of the estate restricted the river, causing a rise in water level that was held back by the construction of levees on the village side.

h2. History

A village grew around a ford on the River Deren. [[House Oestra | House Oestra]] later established a family estate here — manor, bridge toll, and levees to drain and protect the surrounding land. The estate was eventually converted into [[Fallowhurst College | Fallowhurst College]].

Something disastrous happened at the college. The village was ultimately abandoned; at some point the levees failed and the Deren flooded the streets - hence the local name, “The Drowned Crossing.”

The ruins have clearly stood empty for a long time.

On the 25th of Enean 4218 ([[The Soulwind Calendar | SC]]), the [[The Adventurers | Neighborhood Wonch]] passed through the village on their way to [[Sindrel | Sindrel]].[1]

h2. History

Fallowhurst was founded as a small fishing village around a shallow ford of the River Deren — **centuries before the rift** (~**3180 SC** and earlier).

[[House Oestra | House Oestra]] established a family estate at the ford: manor, bridge toll, and levees to drain land for the build. This predates [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] itself. The bridge occupied what had been the ford, which caused local resentment.

The manor was the seat of [[House Oestra | House Oestra]] until ~**3234 SC**, when [[:lord-ablas-oestra | Lord Ablas Oestra]] moved the family seat to [[Sindrel | Sindrel]]. The estate passed to [[:embren-oestra | Embren]], who turned it into [[Fallowhurst College | Fallowhurst College]].

As the college grew, [[:lord-ablas-oestra | Lord Ablas]] pushed ever more ambitious experiments toward immortality. In the operating theatre, Embren and his colleagues attempted a dark binding ritual; it backfired (~**3238 SC**). The scholars were killed instantly.

Following the tragedy, the college closed. The village gradually emptied — largely gone by ~**3288 SC**. Within a decade (~**3298 SC**) the levees failed.

The spell bound the scholars' souls to the body on the operating table. They kidnap **travellers** from [[The Mage Road | the Mage Road]] and perform a macabre parody of magical surgery.

The fresher corpses found in the ruins are not villagers — they are **travellers** taken by the undead scholars still bound to the operating theatre.
