A shabby tavern in [[The Wallarch District | Wallarch]] — cheap spirits, cheaper company, and a back room for patrons who know the password. Deathwalker gambled for information with [[:emerich-surefoot | Emerich Surefoot]] here.[1] When the Wonch later chased him down he claimed his [[The Black Cats | Black Cats]] contact operates from the back room; the word to enter is *Swordfish*.[2]

[[The Adventurers | The Wonch]] later met [[:sable-silvestre | Sable Silvestre]] in the back room while pursuing [[:chuggaz | Chuggaz]] and the Fulcrum sabotage lead.[3]

h3. Exterior

bq). The sign hangs by one rusted chain, which is either disrepair or a philosophical statement about rest. _The Cutthroat's Repose_ is painted in a hand that clearly learned lettering under grudging sufferance - the _o_ in _Repose_ has given up and become a smudge. Beneath it, in smaller letters: _Fine Ales. No Questions. Some Answers._
<br>
The building is [[Sindrel | Sindrel]] stonework gone tired - seamless conjured stone patched with timber and optimism. Overhead, a service bridge cuts the sky in half and explains why the street feels like late afternoon even when it isn't. A pair of [[House Beltus | Beltus]] imps trundle past with a crate of something that clinks like bottles and smells like regret. The iron-banded oak of the door is well maintained in the way of doors that expect to have to win arguments.

h3. Interior

bq). It is a low room - soot-dark beams, tables bolted to the floor, and a bar that has been polished by elbows more than any actual cleaning. The air is thick with cheap spirits, stale pipe smoke, and the faint metallic tang you get when someone nearby has cast a cantrip recently. A dartboard hangs on the far wall, next to a sign reading _NO FIGHTING_ that has been stabbed at least twice by darts that missed the board entirely, and once by what appears to have been an axe.

h3. The Back Room

bq). The noise of the tavern doesn't quite reach this room. It arrives at the walls, thinks better of it, and leaves as a polite murmur. The air smells of old smoke, fresh ink, and damp wood. Nothing here is expensive, but everything is well used, well placed, and exactly where someone would want it when business turns complicated.
<br>
Behind the table sits a woman with the look of someone already pricing your interruption. The ledger before her snaps shut as you enter, the practiced movement of ink-stained fingers performed by somebody for whom discretion is a habit. Her face is carefully forgettable, tiredness sits easily on her shoulders; competence sits easier.
<br>
This is an office in exactly the same way a wolf's den is a waiting room: business gets done here, with an air of menace and predation, and anyone leaving often does so in the impression that they have narrowly escaped with their skin.

h2. Overview

Wallarch dive that functions as a **Black Cats** relay — public room for **Scratches** and embedded **Latches**; back room for a **Tally** ([[:sable-silvestre | Sable Silvestre]]) who holds ledger extracts and adjutant kompromat for **Vault**. Not a headquarters; nobody important commits on premises.

**Address context:** A side street within sight of the [[Quarantine Zone | quarantine wall]] — close enough to smell old iron and ozone on a bad day, far enough that Meness patrols only pass through on schedule.
